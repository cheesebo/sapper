War Diary

 

384th Field Battery RA 142nd Field Regiment RA

 

PLACE

DATE

 

 

Brickwall

Sep 1 1940

No Church Parade owing to Air raid warning as Troops were moving off. Kit & Billet Inspection held.

 

ADS

 

2

Officer’s day held at Brickwall. CRA present during the morning. Beautiful weather still continuing.

 

ADS

 

3

Drill order for Freddie Tp and Edward Tp & Bty staff held in Northiam area. Rather an inconclusive day from the gunners point of view.

 

ADS

 

4

Edward Troop fired their French 75s onto beach tasks at 1700 hrs. Shoot was very successful proving the accuracy of the gun. CRA present throughout the shoot. The CO inspected troops.

 

ADS

 

5

Nothing of interest to report. Another perfect day. Most of the troops arranged bathing parades in the sea.

 

ADS

 

6

Don Troops 3rd gun put into a permanent anti-tank role at Rye Harbour bridge.

 

ADS

 

7

100% manning and stand to ordered for all beach defences as from 2000 hrs today. Considerable air activity & incendiary bombs dropped all around F Tp position - destroying command post & damaging mess room. London heavily raided at night.

 

ADS

 

8

Order received at 2100 hrs. “Extreme vigilance will be kept throughout the night as enemy sea force invasion very probable” signed Admiralty Dover. All Tps stood to throughout the night. Heavy air activity & London bombed again. Church parades held in spite of air raid warnings.

 

ADS

 

9

Close watch along coast again kept. Troops are resting most of their men now by day. Visit to Freddie Troop by Divisional Commander who expressed satisfaction at what he saw.

 

ADS

 

10

Freddie Troops new command post completed. Lt Rowland Jones taken to hospital at Tunbridge Wells with gastric trouble.

 

ADS

 

11

Big air battle during afternoon and many enemy bombers brought down in Rye area. Lewis guns from Freddie & Edward Troops opened fire on enemy machines. An exciting afternoon.

 

ADS

Cresswells Playden

12

BHQ moved to Cresswells Playden. Office BSM & 11 men all told living there at present. Lt Sir William Williams moved with BHQ. Size of BHQ materially reduced by HQ troop being divided amongst the 3 troops.

 

ADS

 

13

Quartermaster & stores moved from Brickwall to Broomhill Lodge, Playden.  MO moved to Cresswells. Weather deteriorated and heavy rain for the first time for several weeks.

 

ADS

 

14

Stand to & manning reduced to 33 1/3 % which will enable the troops to get more sleep and consequently more training throughout the day.

 

ADS

 

15

All troops attended Church Parades. The CO & 2 I/C attending with Freddie Troop at Playden. After Church, the CO inspected the billets at Freddie Troop.

 

ADS

 

16

Lt Head returned to duty after GPOs course at Larkhill followed by a week’s leave. A regimental course for GPOs was started at Playden, this to take place each afternoon with different lectures. A stormy night which made the threatened invasion seem improbable. MO inspected F Troop in morning.

 

ADS

 

17

One of Don Troops British 75s left to go to Colchester, but Don have taken over Edward Troops 5th gun so they are still a complete troop of 4 guns. Segt. Milford-Slade transferred to new defence Regt as BQMS.

 

ADS

 

18

Lt Roland Jones discharged from hospital. Sergt. Abbot transferred to new defence regiment as BQMS. Half holiday & football practice.

 

ADS

 

19

Lt Roland Jones went on 1 week’s sick leave. Troop training.

 

ADS

 

20

Troop Training. Pay parades.

 

ADS

 

21

Inspections & Troop parades. Football (soccer) against RAMC which provided an enjoyable game but an easy win for 384th Bty.

 

ADS

 

22

Church Parades at Playden (F Tp & part Don Tp) & Rye (Edward Tp). Regimental luncheon party at Brickwall ostensibly to say goodbye to Major Mardon, but we heard on arrival that he was to stay with us a further 3 months. Lunch party a great success & to be repeated in future. Scare (Invasion) on about 1600 hrs & 100% stand to ordered. The night however passed off peacefully. Capt Martin returned from sick leave.

 

ADS

 

23

Still 100% manning as scare still on. A quiet day though with no incidents. Lt Robertson appointed “Education Officer” for the Battery.

 

ADS

 

24

Lt Head transferred to HQ 45th Div (AAQMG Dept). Everybody sorry to see him go but wish him the best of luck in his new job. Capt Martin visited the Royal Irish Fusiliers to see beach defences.

 

ADS

 

25

Officers day at Brickwall – Don Troop told that they must definitely find a position in area Bexhill for a troop of French 75s & both their officers went to reconnoitre. Not very successful owing to difficult country.

 

ADS

 

26

F Troop practised firing on Task B2. Most unsatisfactory day as No 2 gun was 1º25’ off for line and as the task was enfilade, the result was very dangerous & nearly disastrous. F Troop & their officers very down about it all & determined not to have any such slip up on Saturday when they fire again. Infantry officers present as spectators.

 

ADS

 

27

Pay Parades & routine drills. Don Troop gave up their positions At Gorse Cottage, Camber Castle & Rye Harbour.

 

ADS

 

28

F Troop again practised firing. This time 20 Rds on Task BII. Shoot was most successful – guns being concentrated on target and then distributed over task. Finished with 1 Rd Salvo which arrived exactly where wanted. Clinometer Laying in the troop and the gun drill was good throughout. Infantry spectators duly impressed. Capt Martin went off again on sick leave.

 

ADS

 

29

Church Parades at Playden, Rye & Brickwall (Don Tp). Found at last a position for Don Troop near Bexhill and they are to move there early next week. Lt Robertson left for CPOs course at Larkhill.

 

ADS

 

30

Troop training and fatigues. E Tp practised firing on SA range at Battle during afternoon. Junior NCOs course started at Brickwall – 384th Bty sending 10 Bdrs & L/Bdrs. GPOs course at Playden PM.

ADS
