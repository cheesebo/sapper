War Diary

 

384th Field Battery RA 142nd Field Regiment RA

 

PLACE

DATE

 

 

Overton

1 Apr 1941

At 0100 hrs A DR from RHQ arrived with a march table for the following morning, and a quick glance at this said that Bty recce parties were not to move before 0700 hrs. The morning was vile as regards weather, heavy sleet & snow falling continuously throughout the day. When preparing the Recce party to move at 0700 hrs a message was received from RHQ at 0650 saying that these parties could now move at 0630 hrs!!! Eventually the Recce party got off at 0655 and had a most unpleasant drive through the snow for about 20 miles. Very little time was allowed for recce as BCs had to report at the Bde Cmdr’s conference at 0745 hrs. Again it is suggested that it would have been better for BCs not to have attended this conference but to have met the Regtl Cmdr at a later hour. CPOs were given areas for the Tps from the 2 I/C and the BC met “O” parties at 0830 and put them in the picture. There was rather a long delay before the guns eventually got into action, owing to blocks of traffic on the road. 383 Bty & their “B” echelon moving just in front of 384. It is suggested that guns being more important should move in front of “B” echelons, and that 383 “B” echelon should have been behind the guns of 384. At 1015 Don Tp reported in action close up to the river in a dual purpose role of A-tank and observed shooting. Not a good position as very exposed. Orders were received about this time that the exercise would finish at 1100 hrs owing to the awful weather. Having been at the Btn HQ of 8th Devons the BC went to the rear HQ at RHQ at 1100 hrs for orders. Zero hour for the return journey was fixed for 1300 hrs and the whole Bty rendezvoused at Charlie Tps position for a hot meal. The return journey began about 1330 hrs and billets were reached about 1600 hrs, the route being through Basingstoke, and Aldermaston. The exercise on the whole was most disappointing, being completely ruined by the shocking weather.

 

ADS

Basildon

2

The Battery returned home from the Winchester area, independent from the remainder of the Brigade as the march table did not arrive in time for the regiment to conform. The Battery arrived back at Basildon about 1500 hrs. Quite heavy snow in the morning.

 

CC

 

3

Troop training in the morning and afternoon. Gun drill and scrimming nets. Cold day.

 

CC

 

4

The battery did gun drill and coming in and out of action on the Downs near Streatley. The coming in and out of action was rather slow at first but by the end of the day there was a great improvement. BC CPO and Command Post Staff proceeded on a signal exercise in the morning. The regiment went into a hide at Easthampstead Park and awaited orders there. Movement began at 1245 and we formed the brigade in column. From the time we started till the time we arrived in the area Great Dunmow, Essex, no messages were received. A few tactical messages were sent up to RHQ on the line of march. Just before Great Dunmow we were ordered to a new concentration area from the one ordered when we were in Easthampstead Park. The new area was Kersey (RHQ) the previous one being Hatfield Forest. We got into the new area about 2130 hrs and heard nothing of the tactical situation until 0930 hrs the next morning. Fine weather the whole exercise.

 

CC

 

5

Maintenance and stables was carried out in the morning. Half holiday in the afternoon. Fine.

 

CC

 

6

Church Parade on the Regimental Parade ground for Church at Lower Basildon 1000 hrs. A cold & dry day.

 

CC

 

7

A Battery drill order was held in the Bradfield-Streatley area. Two positions were occupied and there was an advance. The first position was occupied quickly but the advance to the Second position was slow owing largely to the acute shortage of maps. One Tp was lost for a time. A fine cold day.

 

CC

 

8

Troop training on the Downs coming in and out of action and gun drill. Signallers did a signal lay out passing fire orders under Lt Barnaby. A fine day.

 

CC

 

9

Today was devoted to vehicle maintenance, it having been decided that one day a week must be kept for maintenance. Gunners scrimmed nets and did gun drill. A fine day.

 

CC

 

10

Today was devoted to a Regimental exercise, to exercise Command Posts in aeroplane shoots, Barage orders etc. A very successful day. Fine and cold.

 

CC

 

11

Sports were held in the Regimental Parade ground in the afternoon. 384 Bty scored the highest number of points. A fine day.

 

CC

 

12

A maintenance day. All officers attended a TEWT in the area Thame. 25 pdrs and 75mms sited in an anti-tank role. A fine day.

 

CC

 

13

Church Parade at Lower Basildon Church in the morning after an inspection by the CO on the Regimental Parade ground. A fine day.

 

CC

 

14

Battery spent morning loading for the Bulwark exercise. Regiment moved in the evening to a hide in Hedsor Park. Orders were given to BCs about 0030 hrs for the approach march. The movement table came out late and gave very little time for route cards and orders to be given out. At the last moment BCs and CPOs were ordered to join the Bde R&O GPs and only had 20 minutes to get to the RV 4 miles away. R&O groups arrived in the area NW of Tetsworth without any great delay. OPs and Bty positions were selected. The main body was held up for a considerable time owing to road cratering and diversions and did not arrive in the Bty area until about 1500 hrs 3 hrs late. The Bty area was changed owing to 384 Bty and the 56th Medium Regt being given the same area to deploy their guns. This caused a further delay.

 

CC

 

15

Eventually the Battery came into action and we were brought up from reserve and covered the whole Battalion front. An foo went forward with the 6th Devons. And the left hand OP Capt Robertson went forward on foot with wire supporting the 8th Devons. These OPs remained in touch with the Infantry all night. The Infantry had successfully gained a crossing over the river Thame and the Regiment was ordered to move forward to support a fresh attack. 384 Battery moved at 0700 hrs and was in action by 0830 hrs. The exercise ended at 0930 hrs and the Regiment proceeded home at 1220 hrs. Fine weather throughout the exercise.

 

CC

 

17

Troop training coming in and out of action on the Downs. The drill went well and there was a great improvement. A fine day.

 

CC

 

18

The day was spent in maintenance, advantage being taken of a free day. A fine day.

 

CC

 

19

Gunners gun drill. Drivers maintenance. There was a complete inspection of all vehicles. A fine day.

 

CC

 

20

Ammunition was cleaned and vehicles were maintained. Loading in preparation for camp. Fine.

 

CC

 

21

The Regiment moved to Practice Camp at West Down, and arrived at 1400 hrs. The vehicle spacing on the move was not too good, chiefly owing to the bad hills around Newbury.  A fine day.

 

CC

West Down

22

Troops practised coming in and out of action in the morning. The shooting was late and only three shoots were conducted by Capt Stoddart, Capt Chamberlain and Capt Robertson. There was a move which was rather slow owing to Officers and Acks paying too much attention to accuracy and too little to speed. A fine day with east wind.

 

CC

 

23

384 Bty was on duty and gunners did gun drill in the morning, and gun maintenance and sight testing in the afternoon. East wind.

 

CC

 

24

There was a Battery Drill order and on the first advance (2½ miles) the Battery covered the distance in 29 minutes. A withdrawal was practised and this went smoothly.

 

CC

 

25

The Battery shot on the ranges. Major Chichester, Capt Stoddart, Capt Chamberlain, and Capt Robertson and Lt Head conducted shoots. The report from the IG at the gun end was that the gun drill and command post work was good and quick. The move 3 miles was completed in 30 mins. Fine.

 

CC

 

26

Major Chichester, Capt Stoddart, Capt Chamberlain and Capt Robertson shot today. Gun drill and Command post work again good. Signallers were a bit slow but are improving. A fine day.

 

CC

 

27

Air burst ranging and an air OP shoot were carried out by the Battery. Both were successful especially the air OP which got on to the target with very few rounds. Cold.

 

CC

 

28

This was a Regimental Day. A barrage, and concentrations were fired. The barrage was successful except that watches were not properly synchronised and rounds from one Battery went off before time. 384 Btys rounds were accurate and effective. A cold day.

 

CC

Basildon

29

The Regiment proceeded home at 0900 hrs. The march discipline was a great improvement and the distances were quite good. The guns lagged a bit on the hills, but I think this is inevitable as they cannot keep up the speed.

 

CC

 

30

Maintenance in the morning. Half holiday in the afternoon. Cold East wind.
