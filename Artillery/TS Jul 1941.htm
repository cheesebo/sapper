War Diary

 

384th Field Battery RA 142nd Field Regiment RA

 

PLACE

DATE

 

 

Larkhill

1 Jul 941

Firing started early but 384 were last in the list. No tactical positions were pointed, and there was no movement of guns. A shooting gallery, in fact. Major MacMillan, Capts Stoddart, Chamberlain & Robertson & Lieut Head took shoots, and where in the low camp the guns were good & the OP work doubtful, this time the position was reversed, and it must be recorded that each Tp Command Post made a blot. In fairness it should be noted that the GPOs (Lieuts Rowland Jones & Tr??) were at the OP. When shooting finished – about 1700 hrs – we returned to Basildon independently.

 

 

 

2

Conference on yesterday’s shooting and arrangements under Tp Commanders. Hot weather still continues and swimming is most popular.

 

 

 

3

We were visited this morning by the Div Gen – Maj Gen Morgan & the MGRA (Gen Lund) and one troop was “surprised” during A/Tk shooting, and the officers ditto miniature range. Miniature range was the keynote of the day for officers since they did a further hour and a quarter in the evening for the benefit of some of the Pangbourne Nautical College boys.

 

 

 

4

Tps under Tp commanders. Bty staff exercise for GPOs and Acks under Lt Head.

 

 

 

5

Battery carried out a drill order under direction of RHQ as a “run over the course” for the fire and movement exercise at Larkhill on 7/7. Exercise successful on the whole and many valuable lessons learnt, in particular the importance of having wireless sets accurately tuned before starting out. Half holiday in the afternoon.

 

 

 

6

Sunday. Having been unable to have weekly inspections on Saturday they were held today instead. A small party one officer and 30 ORs attended Regimental Church parade.

 

 

Larkhill

7           0930

Left Basildon for Larkhill. Marched as a battery convoy. March discipline improved. One quad died by the roadside with a blown cylinder head gasket. A persistent and annoying source of trouble with these vehicles.

 

 

1315

Arrived at Larkhill and lay up at Fargo camping ground.

 

 

1400

Fire and movement scheme in Bustard area. Scheme consisted of an advance followed by a withdrawal, in each phase continuous support was provided by leap frogging troops and OPs culminating with the battery in one position, troops linked. Standard of shooting on the whole was good; one air shoot being particularly successful. Movement was satisfactory but can be quicker. Wireless worked well. Amm expended 40 rds HE.

 

 

2020

Last round fired. Returned to Fargo where a hot meal was ready and guns were washed out.

 

 

2115

Left for Basildon, arriving at about 0100 hrs. A good journey, lovely night, moon heavily full.

 

 

Basildon

8

Most of the day spent in cleaning and maintenance. A kit inspection was held at 1730.

 

 

 

9

Used new A/Tk range. Considerable improvement on first. Gives greater range, but tgt should be bigger. Class for “learner drivers” started; to take place daily at 1400 hrs under direction of Capt Stoddart.

 

 

 

10

Battery on duty. Training in lines under Tp arrangements. Heat was continuous.

 

 

 

11

Both troops took their guns out on to the Downs and practised coming into action. Quite good times recorded. One or two delays caused by Quads breaking down. An interesting lecture was given in the evening by M Schofield entitled “The Ambitions of Nazi Germany”.

 

 

 

12

Usual Saturday Inspections of guns and vehicles. Standard of cleanliness and general maintenance on these inspections is improving. Muster parade at 1200 hrs at which BC announced the Battery would shortly be moving.

 

 

 

13

Sunday. No Church parades, day spent on fatigues and general duties. Weather broke in series of thunder storms, which cleared the air considerably.

 

 

 

14

Advance party (Capt Stoddart I/C) leave for new area. Training under troop commanders.

 

 

 

15

C Troop drill order on Berkshire Downs. Practised a rather more deliberate movement than we have been doing of late i.e. withdrawal of a troop from a flank covering posn to main defensive posn. D Tp used A/Tk range.

 

 

 

16

Battery on duty. Few men left for training. Officers did outdoor miniature range on Downs; rather restricted by bad weather.

 

 

 

17

BC, Tp Cmdrs, & BSM visited new area and found it not quite so desolate as it had appeared on the map. Troop posns are to be some 5000 yds apart. Administration likely to be the main problem. Bty OP on scaffolding tower generally voted to be very frightening.

 

 

 

18

D tp carried out drill order as for C tp on 15th. Line sigs are obviously in need of practice, as we have been using wireless almost exclusively of late. Heavy rain most of the day.

 

 

 

20

Sunday. Day spent in packing for departure to new area. The time at which the train was to be loaded was altered 11 times during the course of the day.

 

 

 

21

The Battery moved off at 0830 and proceeded in a Brigade column to lying up area near Great Canfield, a distance of about 100 miles. Arrived about 1630. Casualties on the road: “H” truck had brake trouble resulting in brake drum rivets being sheared off, one quad had petrol stoppage due to air inlet on filter cap being painted over.

 

 

 

22

Moved from lying up area to new area near Tillingham, Essex at 0700 hrs arriving about 1100 hrs. Took over from 351 Bty of 88 Fd Regt. Two tp posns, C Tp at Asheldam, D Tp near Bradwell. Both Tps have task of firing on beaches in peninsular between R. Blackwater and R. Crouch and aerodrome at Bradwell. Tps being so far apart produces several administrative problems. Rations have to be collected from RHQ about 20 miles back at Wickford and delivered to Y messes in the Bty; a total journey of

 

 

Tillingham

23

Day spent settling in to new area. Accommodation appears to be adequate and generally speaking we take quite a good view of our new home.

 

 

 

24

Petrol-less day, also later transpired it should have been telephone-less day as well. Visited by 2 I/C Major Mardon with news of possible new dispositions: An additional battery may be brought into the area, necessitating a re-shuffle of troop posns.

 

 

 

25

Recce carried out to find new troop posns. Very difficult to reconcile concealment and a good A/Tk field of fire in this flat country. BCs conference at RHQ in the evening to discuss future events, training etc.

 

 

 

26

Rained hard most of the morning. Bty tug-o-war and relay team practised for Regtl sports meeting to be held at Southend on Saturday next.

 

 

 

27

Sunday. 20 men of BHQ and D Troop attended service at Tillingham Church.

 

 

 

28

Training under Troop arrangements. Under great conditions of uncertainty it is very difficult to run anything in the nature of a progressive training programme.

 

 

 

29

BCs conference at RHQ at which it is broken to us that the Battery is to be split up. C Tp will move to posn near Southminster and come under operational command of a new bty coming into the area. BHQ & D Troop to come back to area Cold Norton latter to cover Latchingdon Gap with DF fire and be prepared to support 8 Devons in counterattack role. Posns to be ready for occupation by 31st.

 

 

 

30

Recce of new posns carried out. Quite good gun posns found but accommodation available nil, tentage will have to be used until huts are ready. Occupation postponed until evening of 1 Aug.

 

 

 

31

Day spent preparing new posns and arranging for necessary tentage and accommodation stores. This entails much rushing about and we are horrified to find that we have already used 330 galls out of our weekly allotment of 400 galls of petrol.
