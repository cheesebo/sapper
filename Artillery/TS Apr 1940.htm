War Diary

 

384th Field Battery RA 142nd (RDY) Field Regiment RA

 

PLACE

DATE

 

 

Okehampton

Apr 1 1940

Troop training in the morning, Baths in the afternoon. “E” Troop did night occupation in the evening. Only four officers owing to leave and courses.  A dull day with slight rain.

 

CC

 

2

Troop training in the morning. Officers did silent registration in the afternoon.

 

CC

 

3

The Battery was inspected by the Commander in Chief at 1015 hrs, while the Battery Staff did a lay out, “E” troop Gun drill and “F” troop PT. A fine day.

 

CC

 

4

The Battery did a drill order in the Exbourne area. Two positions were occupied. A wet day.

 

CC

 

5

Troop training in the morning. Battery Staff were lectured on points from the drill order.

 

CC

 

6

PT and Troop training in the morning. Half holiday in the afternoon.

 

CC

 

7

Church parade at 0940. The preacher was the Rev Compton. The regiment were inspected by the CO at 0920. A good parade. Fine day.

 

CC

 

8

Troop training in the morning. Officers did stage IV under IG Capt Studley in the afternoon.

 

CC

 

9

Drill order under IG. An FOO was sent out and the Battery  advanced by using one troop at a time. A most instructive day and fine.

 

CC

 

10

The Regiment was inspected by the C in C Southern Command. The Battery were doing lamp reading, Gun drill in gas masks  and PT. Officers did stage IV practical in the morning under the IG. A fine day.

 

CC

 

11

Troop training in the morning. Gunners did visual training in the afternoon. A very fine day.

 

CC

 

12

Troop training in the morning. All officers did a TEWT in the Sheepwash district with the IG. A most successful day. Slight rain in the morning.

 

CC

 

13

Route march in the morning. A fine day.

 

CC

 

14

Church parade at 0940 hrs at the Parish Church. Preacher was the Rev Compton.

 

CC

 

15

E Troop proceeded on a billeting exercise at North Tawton, the first exercise of its kind. Troop training in the morning and afternoon.  A dull day.

 

CC

 

16

Troop training in morning. Battery staff did a lay out on the Gun Park. A wet day. Visited F troop on their billeting exercise who had made themselves very comfortable.

 

CC

 

17

Muster Parade and full kit inspection in the morning.

CC

 

18

Troop training in the morning. E Tp returned from their Billeting exercise in the afternoon. A fine day.

 

CC

 

19

PT and Troop training in the morning. Pay parade and individual training in the afternoon.

 

CC

 

20

A route march for the Battery in respirators  in the morning. “D” Troop had a muster parade in preparation for the Billeting exercise. A fine day.

 

CC

 

21

Church parade at 0940 hrs at the Parish Church. Preacher the Rev Compton. A very good parade. A fine day.

 

CC

 

22

“D” Troop proceeded on a Billeting exercise to Chagford and Moretonhampstead. Troop training. Drivers were running in motor cycles which arrived on Friday. A fair day.

 

CC

 

23

Battery Staff Exercise with E Tp. Drivers were running in Motor cycles.

 

CC

 

24

“E” Troop did outdoor map reading in the morning. Lectures for Nos 1 in “MT” were started at 1730 hrs. A fine day. I visited D Troop at Moretonhampstead who had a good programme of work.

 

CC

 

25

E Tp did 18 Pdr gun drill in the morning. F Tp did a route march in respirators. Running in new motor cycles. D Tp returned from Billeting exercise.

 

CC

 

26

Cars have been allotted to Troops and “Stables” are being held daily at 0930 hrs. F Troop occupation of positions. E Tp night occupation with guns night drive 2045.

 

CC

 

27

Route march with respirators. Gas drill and rifle drill for employed men. A fine day.

 

CC

 

28

Church Parade at Parish Church 0940 hrs. Preacher Rev Compton. A wet day.

 

CC

 

29

“F” Tp proceeded on a Billeting exercise to Chagford and Moretonhampstead. A fine morning. E Tp did a drill order on the moor. A most successful morning. A wet afternoon.

 

CC

 

30

Battery staff did a Barage in the morning. Troop training. Lt Roland Jones and myself did an exercise with the 6th Devons. Most instructive.

CC
