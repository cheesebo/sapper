War Diary

 

384th Field Battery RA 142nd Field Regiment RA

 

PLACE

DATE

 

 

Playden nr. Rye Sussex

1 Oct 1940

Don Troop set about digging gun positions between Cooden and Bexhill. About midday it was learnt that this position was to be allotted to B troop. Lt Sir W. Williams and 2 NCOs attended a demonstration of revetting by the REs at Battle.

CC

 

 

 

 

 

2

Officer’s day took place at Brickwall House, Northiam in the morning. This time a number of NCOs per troop attended.  A half holiday in the afternoon. Vegetables and fruit are still being collected from certain civilians at Northiam by the Welfare Officer. The generosity of these individuals having done much to alleviate a shortage of green stuffs noticeable in the supply of rations.

 

CC

 

3

F Troops guns were withdrawn by the 100th Field Regiment, much to the sorrow of all ranks in this troop who had worked hard to achieve a model position and had acquired that affection for their weapons inherent in all good gunners.

 

WL Williams

 

4

A filthy wet day with a dank misty rain blowing from the west – In spite of this lone enemy aircraft paid us periodical visits, and bombs were dropped near the Rye Golf Club House in the morning between the Estuary mouth and Camber Castle in the afternoon, while a few incendiaries fell on the marsh to the right of the Winchelsea Road in the evening. No serious damage was done, though two civilians and the bar stock in the golf club sustained slight damage from the morning raid. Freddie troop moved from Playden to take up their new position beyond Rye on the Winchelsea Road. Don and Edward continued individual training under their troop commanders particular attention being paid to anti-gas drill and spray from the air. 2/Lt Barnaby attended a demonstration by the REs in tree felling during the afternoon, with a view to revetting our slit and other trenches.

 

WL Williams

 

5

In the morning most officers and 10 ORs per troop went to Ninfield to witness a display of “Dive Bombing”. HQ staff moved out of Cresswells and went to new billets in Playden village. Major C. Chichester rejoining from attending a course at the 125th Officers Cadet Training Unit and took over the command of the Bty from Capt AD Stoddart.

 

 

 

6

Don Tp and HQs attended Church Parade at Iden and E Tp and F Tp at Rye. A fine day, a few showers.

 

CC

 

7

Troop training in the morning and afternoon. Junior leaders course in the morning and a GPOs course in the afternoon. Signalling under Sgt Harman. Showers. Capt Stoddart proceeded on a week’s sick leave.

 

CC

 

8

Battery Staff skeleton drill order, with D and E Troops. F Tp continued constructing gun emplacements on Rye – Winchelsea Road. There was a Battery Commander conference in the afternoon at Mountfield. A fine day.

 

CC

 

9

Troop training in morning and afternoon. All officers attended lectures at Brickwall in the morning. D Tp and E Tp shot on the open range at Battle in the afternoon. A showery day. 18 bombs were dropped in and around Rye.

 

CC

 

10

Major General ECA Schreiber DSO inspected the billets, stores and cookhouses of the Battery during the day. Troop training in the morning and afternoon. A wet day.

 

CC

 

11

Troop training in the morning. Battery Commanders, Battery Captains and WLOs Indoor exercise at Brickwall. Regimental movement exercise. A fine day.

 

CC

 

12

Troop training in the morning. Games in the afternoon.

 

CC

 

13

Church Parade for D Tp at Iden, E & F Tps at Rye. A fine day.

 

CC

 

14

Troop training in the morning and afternoon. All officers attended lectures by Brigadier de Fonblanque at Normanhurst Court. Captain Chamberlain proceeded on leave. A dull day.

 

CC

 

15

Troop training in the morning and afternoon. A fine day.

 

CC

 

16

All officers attended lectures at Normanhurst Court. Troop training in morning and afternoon.

 

CC

 

17

Troop training in morning and afternoon. Don Tp began sandbagging billets to a height of 3ft from ground. A fine day.

 

CC

 

18

All officers attended lectures at Normanhurst Court. Troop training in morning and afternoon. A dull day.

 

CC

 

19

Inspections and troop training in morning and organised games in the afternoon.

 

CC

 

20

D Tp attended Church at Iden. E and F Tps attended Church at Rye. A fine day.

 

CC

 

21

Troop training in the morning and the afternoon. Lt Johnson proceeded on leave. A fine day. All officers attended lectures at Normanhurst Court.

 

CC

 

22

Troop training in morning and afternoon. A fine day.

 

CC

 

23

All officers attended lectures at Normanhurst Court. Troop training in the morning and afternoon. Captain Martin proceeded to Tunbridge Wells Hospital. A fine day.

 

CC

 

24

E Tp did a troop drill order in the morning. A fine day.

 

CC

 

25

All officers attended lectures at Normanhurst Court in the morning. Captain Chamberlain returned from leave and sick leave. A fine day.

 

CC

 

26

Troop training and inspections in the morning. Games in the afternoon. A fine day.

 

CC

 

27

Church Parade at Iden & Rye. There was a conference at RHQ concerning the new organisation of the Regiment. A fine day.

 

CC

 

28

Troop training in the morning and afternoon. Lt Johnson returned from leave. A fine but cold day.

 

CC

 

29

There was a Brigade movement exercise all day in which the Battery took part. 384th Battery was leading the Brigade Column. A fine day.

 

CC

 

30

Troop training in morning and afternoon. All troops of the Battery practised a hand over for the hand over which is to take place with the 275 Fd Bty next week. A fine day.

 

CC

 

31

Troop training in morning and afternoon. Major Mews went round the Battery in the afternoon.  A very wet day.

CC
