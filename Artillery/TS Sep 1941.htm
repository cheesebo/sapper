War Diary

 

384th Field Battery RA 142nd Field Regiment RA

 

PLACE

DATE

 

 

Larkhill

1 Sep 1941

Troops practised “quick actions” in the morning, and the Battery did a “fire and movement” exercise in the evening. 1730-2015 hrs. Shooting quick and accurate, movement quite good. Some unnecessary delays caused by bad wireless discipline and procedure.

 

 

 

2

Battery returned independently to Essex. Very good journey. Left Larkhill 0800 hrs arrived Cold Norton 1715 hrs. Hustled through London with amazing efficiency by Metropolitan Police

 

 

Cold Norton

3

Maintenance and cleaning after camp at Larkhill. Arrangements are being made to get us some huts from Clacton on Sea. These will be very welcome for winter accommodation, but their dismantling, transport and erection is going to take a lot of time and labour.

 

 

 

4

Petrol-less day. Work was to have started on huts at St Osyth near Clacton but recce party of Tp cmdrs sent out on 3 Sep report that labour problem is likely to be even more difficult than was at first expected. Infantry already on the job find that it takes 20 men one day to dismantle one hut. Decide that the only solution is to send the greater part of D Tp complete i.e. about 60 men, to live at Osyth while the work is in progress. This cannot be done without operational sanction.

 

 

 

5

Both troops used A-Tk range in the morning. 134 Inf Bde have promised to provide 6 Pioneers with tools to assist in dismantling of huts. Permission given for party of 60 men from D Tp & RHQ to proceed to Clacton on Sunday 7 Sept. They will have to return for exercise “Gun” 12-13 Sept.

 

 

 

6

A drizzling wet and rather ??? day. Nothing of importance to record.

 

 

 

7

Party from D Tp and BHQ went off to St Osyth under Capt. Chamberlain to start work on huts. National Day of Prayer observed by a small party of the ??? attending Cold Norton Church.

 

 

 

8

News received from hut party that work has been stopped by order of CRA because huts to be taken down were in vicinity of a OP. Eventually decided that four huts should be left on either side of OP to preserve appearance.

 

 

 

9

C Tp fired on target on the coast to obtain data for engaging DF tasks. Results satisfactory and showed that guns were shooting well together and appeared to be accurately calibrated. Work on huts proceeding satisfactorily. They are being dismantled at the rate of 2-3 per day.

 

 

 

10

C Tp and remnants of BHQ and D Tp fired on Middle Wick .303 ranges. Results not very good possibly due to rifles not having been properly zeroed.

 

 

 

11

Nothing much doing. Owing to petrol-less day no huts were delivered today.

 

 

 

12

C Tp and some of the remnants of D Tp used the A-Tk range in the morning. This range is providing excellent training but tracer amn is essential and we are running very short of it.

 

 

 

13

We were visited in the afternoon by the Lord Lieutenant Sir Francis Whitmore who came to see if we were comfortable. He foresees that we shall be much troubled with mud and wet in the winter, and has promised to do what he can to stir up the Garrison Engineer on our behalf.

 

 

 

14

Sunday. Morning spent in preparation for exercise “Guns”. Moved off in drizzling rain at 1600 hrs. Lay up for night near Hailstead.

 

 

 

15

Exercise began in earnest at 0500 hrs. The battery supporting 8 Devons in forward body role. Several quick actions successfully. Wireless bad at beginning owing to bad tuning. No 18 sets being used for communication with infantry worked well after a shaky start. This method of calling fire should prove very successful with practice. Methods of indicating targets requires considerable practice in liaison with infantry. The advance was held up near Chelmsford in the afternoon. Whole regiment deployed near Pleshey to support Brigade attack. Most of the evening spent getting into view posns, dumping ammunition and working out data for barrage to support attack.

 

 

 

16

Attack goes in at 0500 hrs supported by barrage for 30 minutes. FOOs went forward with leading infantry and ?? by observation keeping in touch with inf comds by No 18 sets which worked well. Returned to Cold Norton 1030 hrs.

 

 

 

17

Day spent in maintenance and cleaning.

 

 

 

18

Party went to St Osyth to complete collection of huts. We understand that we are to get additional Nissen huts to replace beach huts disallowed by CRA.

 

 

 

19

C Tp used A-Tk range. Visited by OME who gave guns a brief examination.

 

 

 

20

Tp Cmds conference to discuss progress of hutting arrangements. We are now to get 6 Nissen huts which should make us fairly comfortable. D Tp defeated local A-Tk troop at football.

 

 

 

21

BC attended conference on Ex guns at Div HQ. On the whole the exercise appears to have been a success and the regt got a good chit.

 

 

 

22

Party from D Tp go to St Osyth to complete removal of huts. Min range in the evening for officers going to Larkhill for observation of fire course during the week.

 

 

 

23

3 officers left for Larkhill to shoot. D Tp spent the morning on A-Tk range firing rifles and practising use of Quad winching gear; one quad was effectively ditched, almost given up, but was eventually extracted.

 

 

 

24

Routine work on erecting huts occupied most of the day. These require a lot of labour, as foundations made with concrete are necessary.

 

ADS

 

25

Major FG MacMillan left to umpire the “Bumper” exercise and Capt AD Stoddart took over command of the Battery. Inspection of vehicles at the troops & BHQ took place during the morning. Officers who had been shooting at Larkhill returned having had a successful day’s shooting

 

ADS

 

26

Capt Stoddart & 2/Lt Trost left for Larkhill to shoot on the Saturday and were joined there by 2/Lt Barnaby & 2/Lt Mortimer. Work on huts continued.

 

ADS

 

27

Maintenance, inspections and work on huts filled the morning. Capt Chamberlain left for his course at Larkhill.

 

ADS

 

28

Holy Communion at Cold Norton Church by the Rev Meek at 0815 was well attended by BHQ and Don Troop. Consequently only a few attended the 1100 hrs service. The Larkhill party returned after a successful day’s shooting. All the party were most impressed by the way in which 383 Bty’s guns responded to the orders from the OPs and were rather envious at 383s good fortune in obtaining such excellent practice. It is hoped that 384 will get the next opportunity. 2/Lt Clarke returned from his course.

 

 

 

29

A wet day – the first for over a fortnight, and its to be hoped that this will not mean the weather has broken. ABCA discussion for BHQ was held in the dining hall and produced many questions which augurs well for future talks. WOs class at RHQ started today.

 

ADS

 

30

Wireless class under 2/Lt Barnaby for officers started this morning. The CO visited BHQ and Don Troop. Aerial photographs had revealed gaps in camouflage at Don Troop and steps were taken to put this right. Medical inspection for the whole Battery by the MO during the afternoon.

ADS
