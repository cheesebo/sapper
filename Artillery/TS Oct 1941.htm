War Diary

 

384th Field Battery RA 142nd Field Regiment RA

 

PLACE

DATE

 

 

Cold Norton

1 Oct 1941

The weather still continuing fine, work was continued on the huts. A party under 2/Lt Mortimer went to Bartlow to collect the 5 Nissen huts allotted to the Bty. These have been appointed: 3 to Charlie Troop – 1 to Don Troop and 1 for a BHQ and Don Tp Sgts mess. Wireless exercise for officers during the afternoon. The CRA visited Charlie Troop during the morning and told us to indent for extra telephone wire. Gun stores were handed over by the BQMS.

 

 

 

2

Another good day and work on huts, maintenance and inspection of vehicles, and harvesting occupied most of the day. Many farmers are availing themselves of the opportunity to get Army labour & vehicles to help them gather a very late harvest.

 

 

 

3

Again very little training accomplished as the winter accommodation problem of putting up the huts is considered to be of first importance. Wireless lecture for officers on “faults” took place at BHQ. Regtl Audit board at RHQ at which all Bty accounts were audited. Major FG Macmillan returned from ex Bumper and resumed command of the Battery

 

ADS

 

FM

 

4

Turning rather wet and unpleasant. The sooner we can get our new winter accommodation fixed, the better. Morning spent working on huts and cleaning up new guns.

 

 

 

5

Sunday. BHQ and D Tp attended service at Cold Norton Church. Weather rather depressing.

 

FM

 

6

Most of our efforts are concentrated on teaching ourselves gun drill on our new Mk II guns. They are rather heavier than the Mk I but the provision of a platform is a great asset as is the new type telescopic sight for A-Tk shooting.

 

 

 

7

A message was received asking for volunteers to fill vacancies for Officers in the Middle East. All eligible officers in the battery put their names in. A dense mist in the morning cleared up in the afternoon.

 

FM

 

8

Thick mist prevented possibility of using A-Tk range by C Tp. More gun drill. Drill for inf cooperation using No 18 R/T set tried out successfully at RHQ ??? range Brig Mitchelmore Comd 134 Inf Bde was present and approved suggested procedure.

 

FM

 

9

Poured rain all day. BSM (IG) Holt visited D Tp and gave some very useful instruction in gun drill with Mk II gun. Bad weather stops work on huts.

 

FM

 

10

Rained all day. Mud becoming a big problem again. BSM Holt took C Tp in gun drill. He should have been available for us all next week but has been sent on leave.  Message arrived at 2100 hrs asking for 1 Dvr 1 Sig & 2 guns to go overseas to report to RHQ by 0900 hrs 11 Oct.

 

 

 

11

Weather still poor. Tp Comds conference at 0930 hrs. Gun park inspections under tp arrangements.

 

FM

 

12

Weather improved. Fine but still cold. Visited by Corps camouflage officer who gave some useful hints for disguising gun posns. 3 more men ordered overseas.

 

FM

 

13

Orders received for Lt ESN Head to join draft for overseas

 

FM

 

14

D Tp used A-Tk range. No tracer amn. Fired at stationary tgt with ball?? Amn for grouping practice. Visibility poor.

 

FM

 

15

Under new order issued by GOC 45 Div afternoon was devoted to recreation instead of training.

 

 

 

16

Petrol-less day: nothing much doing. Training is being seriously hampered by lack of men. Some are at RHQ on guard or courses, some doing agricultural work for local farmers.

 

 

 

17

C Tp unable to use A-Tk range due to lack of men. Two new officers joined. 2/Lt Mills & 2/Lt Townsend, posted to C and D Troops respectively. 2/Lt Clarke attached to RHQ as duty officer.

 

FM

 

18

Offr and 5 DRs went to Fingeringhoe to shoot A-Tk  rifle on range there.  Weather turned very stormy and unpleasant.

 

 

 

19

Sunday. BHQ and D Tp attended Harvest festival service in Cold Norton Church. Bde Chaplain, Rev Meek, preached sermon.

 

 

 

20

Fine day. Men much in demand by farmers, a few left for training. Information received of a party arriving to take over 18/25 pdr guns for 24 Fd Regt.

 

 

 

21

C Tp used A-Tk range under supervison of AIG. Lecture in afternoon by Sgt of Security Police on “identity cards and passes”. Officer, OME and NCOs of 23 Fd Regt checked and took over 18/25 pdrs. Guns to remain with us until sent away by rail.

 

 

 

22

D Tp used A-Tk range in the morning. In the afternoon there was a football match between two sides in the battery; and a X country run for runners under 2/Lt Barnaby.

 

 

 

23

Weather turning very cold. Several men of C & D tps still living in tents; no extra blankets and no heating stoves provided. Letter sent to CO and MO pointing out unsatisfactory conditions of accommodation. ABCA lectures and discussions held under Tp arrangements in the evening prove very successful. Concert produced by men of RHQ turned out great success.

 

 

 

24

Manning ex held for BHQ and D Tp held, to practice occupation of Palepit Farm Posn. Several valuable lessons brought out, in particular lack of men and rifles for local protection.

 

 

 

25

Morning spent on officers gun drill, tactical lecture and inspections of guns. Football match in afternoon, Bty team defeated A Coy 8 Devons 8-0.

 

 

 

26

Sunday. 18 men and 1 DR acted as casualties for local ARP exercise. Remainder attended Church at Cold Norton.

 

 

 

27

Parades and training under tp arrangements. Weather turning very cold. Strong, Northerly wind. Some signs that agitation about inadequacy of winter accommodation is having some effect, mainly due to efforts of ??? MO.

 

 

 

28

The Battery had the use of Middlewick rifle ranges for the morning. 20 men per tp fired rifle course and Bren & Tommy guns were also fired. Results poor.

 

 

 

29

D Tp did a drill order in the morning and C Tp used A-Tk range. Football in the afternoon, Bty  team defeated 383 Bty 3-2. Weather very poor. Some snow fell.

 

 

 

30

Petrol-less day, devoted as usual to inspection of vehicles etc. Some ?dvrs who have been undergoing training to qualify as Drvrs I C were tested by Capt Stoddart.

 

 

 

31

Battery drill order in morning. Practised support of advancing infantry. Very short of guns. Many of the new 25 pdrs are suffering from “teething troubles” mostly in connection with leaking buffers.
