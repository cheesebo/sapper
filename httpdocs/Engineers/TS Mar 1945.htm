War Diary

 

158th Railway Construction Company Royal Engineers

 

PLACE

DATE

 

 

 

Hemiksem

2 Mar 45

2305

Rocket Bomb (V2) incident in sidings of South Stn, Antwerp MR J.662945. Work carried out continuously by shifts until complete at 1800 hrs 3 Mar 45. Twenty ORs from Sec 4 att f.a.p. to 162 Rly Constr Coy RE for work at Oosterweel in 107 TnSD

 

 

6

1000

Meeting at Antwerp Central Stn between SNCFB Officials and Lt Col Scott RCE6, Major Rutledge OC 164 Rly Optg Coy RE and Maj Millard were also present. It was agreed that 158 Coy should

(1)    Assist in repairs to 15 turnouts and track in Esschen Yard

(2)    Handover the maintenance of main line from Kapellen to Roosendaal

(3)    Lay 6 new sidings and approx 2 ½ miles track at Schijnpoort Yard

Lt Col Scott, Maj Millard and M. Wijffels of SNCFB visited Esschen

 

 

 

7

0200

Rocket bomb incident on sidings in petrol installation at Hoboken MR J643944. Repairs commenced at 0800 hrs and proceeded continuously till complete at 1500 hrs

 

 

 

8

1730

Flying bomb incident (V1) on main line Antwerp-Lierre at Boschoek MR J763875. Repairs commenced at 2300, and proceeded continuously until complete. Handed back to traffic at 1040 hrs on 9 Mar.

 

 

8

2130

Flying bomb incident (V1) at Esschen. Direct hit on café 50 yds from Sec 4 billet killing all occupants, 9 civilians and 5 military. Casualties this unit  10534899 Spr Sykes N and 2020561 Spr Kinley J, killed. 903043 Spr Cape J wounded in adjacent house and removed to hospital with broken arm and shock. Sec 4 billet badly blasted.

 

 

 

10

1400

Section 4 moved from Esschen to Kappellen MR 712067 and att f.a.p. 91 Coy PC

 

 

 

11

1200

Officer commanding, Maj Millard left for 48 hrs leave Bruges

 

 

 

12

0800

Work on 6 new sidings at Schijnpoort Yard commenced by Sections 1 and 2

 

 

 

13

1800

Maj Millard returned from 48 hrs leave Bruges

 

 

 

15

 

Rocket Bomb incident (V2) Schijnpoort Garden Sidings (not on new work). Repairs commenced at 0940 hrs. Completed as far as this unit concerned & handed over to Belgian Rly Authorities at 0800 hrs 16 Mar 45

Lt Col WJ Scott OC 6 Rly Constr & Maint Gp moves to forward area together with 956 Rly Svy Coy and 162 Rly Constr Coy OC 158 Rly Constr Coy left as officer i/c Rly Constr Antwerp Area.

Flying bomb incident (V1) on main electric line Brussels-Antwerp at Vieux Dieu Stn. Work commenced 0620. No military traffic involved. Handed back to Belgium Rly Authorities at 0755 hrs

CONFIDENTIAL

Subject: EMERGENCY REPAIRS

 

Dear Millard

 

I wish to let you know how pleased I was at the way your men tackled the recent incident at Hoboken.

 

The men seemed to be in good form, and the work was well organised and quite up to the high standard which has already been set by the 603 Coy dealing with similar incidents.

 

I hope you do not have too many of these incidents to deal with, but no doubt your men have now got the hang of the job and will put up a good show.

 

                                                            Yours

                                               W.J.Scott

 

Lt Col RE

CO No 6 Rly Constr and Maint Group RE

BLA

8 Mar 45
