FJ Millard

 

    

To O.C. 158th Railway Construction Coy RE

Subject: Move Lisieux to Kalmthout

From: Lieut HG Bamford RE (O i/c Train Party)

 

(1)     At 1200 hrs 21 Dec 44 I received from you warning order that I would be O i/c Train party in Company move from Lisieux to Kalmthout. The information was that the train would carry 206 Rly Workshop Coy RE and elements of 125 Quarrying Coy RE  the train to start from Caen and arrive Lisieux at 1200 hrs approx 22 Dec 44

(2)     On 22 Dec 44 at 1100 hrs the Rail Party was ready to move away to Lisieux Station. At 1600 hrs No 1 Cdn Rly Op Coy telephoned information that the train had just then left Caen and should arrive Lisieux about 1730 hrs. The rail party was immediately marched to the station. The train arrived at 1815 hrs approx.

(3)     On enquiries being made it was found that no official stops had been allocated by MC for feeding etc and that feeding would have to be fitted in with halts and pauses in journey caused by traffic exigencies. Thus in spite of impossibility of effectively cooking meals en route, no official times for providing meals could be laid down.

(4)     The train left Lisieux at approx 1845 hrs

(5)     The train running on night of 22 Dec and day 23 December 44 was good and meals were effectively fitted in with stops for engine changing, or for engine water.

(6)     The train arrived at Brussels at 0200 hrs 24 Dec 44, and was shunted into a siding, where it remained stationery till 1000 hrs 24 Dec 44

(7)     Train arrived outside limits of Antwerp Station (East) at 1500 hrs, where it awaited signals until 1600 hrs. By then I was worried about not moving so suggested to the Belgian driver that he should walk to the signal box. At 1645 hours we moved forward about a quarter of a mile, when we were halted again outside Schijnpoort Yard. I then went to the next signal cabin with the guard, who telephoned the Belgian Control at Schijnpoort Sidings. The guard informed that the office did not know our train had arrived at Antwerp. We were moved forward into a siding at Schijnpoort sidings at 1715 hrs.

(8)     I then went forward to speak to the RTC and to contact, if possible, our own company HQ by telephone. This was not possible, but whilst I was there Capt Roulstone of RCE came through on the RTO telephone. I asked if unit transport could be sent to Antwerp to carry my party to Kalmthout, 10 miles out of Antwerp, and so obviate the possibility of spending a further night on the train. This could have been arranged to be effected by 1830 hrs, but when Major Leigh of Movement Control was contacted he refused permission for the party to detrain, and ordered that they would remain on the train until arrival at Kalmthout, even though there seemed to be no immediate possibility of the train leaving Antwerp.

(9)     On my return to the train at 1800 hrs I found that the Belgian Engine crew had taken away their engine on orders from some military personnel, who I was unable to trace. This I reported to RTO and he made arrangements for the first engine available to be hooked up to our train. A corporal of 164 Rly Optg Coy RE put the instructions for bringing the train from the shed to the Belgian Control at approx 1845 hrs. By 1945 hrs no engine had arrived, I therefore saw the corporal of the 164 Coy again and he checked up and found that the engine had not left the shed, but would do so immediately. At about a quarter of an hour later information was given me that the engine had left the shed and should arrive at our train position in five minutes.

(10)   About 20 minutes later the engine had still not arrived so I spoke to Belgian Control direct. They had no knowledge of the engine having left the sheds. After some argument the Belgian Control did some urgent telephoning and at approx 2045 hrs the engine was hooked up to our train ready to go.

(11)   At the end of a further half hour no signal to move had been given so I went forward to the 164 ROC corporal and after further enquiries it appeared that all lines out of Schijnpoort Yard to Antwerp North were blocked by:-

(1)     A derailment spread over 2 tracks

(2)     An American train of empties stuck on a 3rd track, unable to move for lack of steam

(3)     A smash between an American and a Belgian train just South of Antwerp North

(12)   I was now assured that our train would now very probably rest at Schijnpoort for some hours and I therefore telephoned RCE6 at approx 2200 hrs for unit tpt. Col Scott telephoned Movement Control and obtained authority for my party to detrain at once. Authority was given and immediately acted upon; a small guard being left on the train to protect rations till they could be unloaded at Kalmthout. Unit tpt arrived at Midnight, and my party were in camp by 0230 25 Dec 1944.

(13)   It has since been reported to me by the Sgt that I left on the train that the train moved forward from Schijnpoort Yard at about 2400 hrs and arrived in Antwerp North at 0630 hrs, 25 Dec 44, it was there held up because of traffic jam No 3 described above. The English Optg corporal pointed out to Belgian Control that the train could be shunted through a cross over road and detour the jam in an operation taking about 10-15 minutes. This the Belgians would not do. At 1200 hrs approx an American train was in fact shunted through that way and proceeded before our personnel train. The Operating Cpl again approached the Belgian control and after some argument our train took the cross-over route as originally suggested. Antwerp North was departed 1300 hrs and the train arrived in Kalmthout at 1400 hrs 25 Dec 44. Here 158 Rly Construction Coy wagons were detached from personnel train.

(14)   From the above it will be seen that from time of arrival in Brussels to arrival in Antwerp was 12 hours. Antwerp to Kalmthout (approx 20 km) was a journey of 24 hours. I was assured at Antwerp that this is not unusual. Further I would state that every effort possible was made by the English operating Coy to clear our train as quickly as possible, and the delays seemed to be caused largely by reluctance of the Belgian Control to co-operate effectively.

 

 

31 Dec 44                                                      HG Bamford  Lt RE

158th Railway Construction RE
