War Diary

 

384th Field Battery RA 142nd Field Regiment RA

 

PLACE

DATE

 

 

Tillingham

1 Aug 1941

Day spent in preparation for moves to new positions. Tents arrive just in time. Weather luckily remains fine.

 

 

1900

Positions left, and BHQ & D Tp move to Cold Norton. C Tp to Southminster.

 

 

 

2

Day spent in settling in to new posns, and preparing data of new operational roles. Bty sports team to Southend in the afternoon where a notable success was scored against other btys in tug-o-war and relay races, scoring 14 pts out of a possible 15.

 

 

 

3

Sunday. Continue with process of settling-in. Tps quite comfortable, weather continues fine. We shall probably have trouble with vehicle standings in wet weather.

 

 

 

4

Training and work on new posns carried out under troop arrangements. Turned wet and blowing hard in the evening, but all tents still standing.

 

 

 

5

Blowing hard with occasional showers throughout the day. 5 Officers depart for Larkhill on short observation of fire course. Tps to take shoots, others to watch.

 

 

 

6

Training proceeded under Troop arrangements with Battery classes for learner drivers and Acks.

 

 

 

7

Work proceeded at troop positions. Inspection of MT carried out by Battery Commander.

 

 

 

8

Usual pay parade & continuation of defence works with individual training of specialists.

 

 

 

9

Major MacMillan left for leave and Capt AD Stoddart assumed command during his absence. The technical training fortnight continued during the morning with inspections of vehicles, signal stores and guns and this was followed by lecture & practical working of meteor telegrams, which showed the need for more practice in this subject. Half holiday.

 

 

 

10

BHQ & Don Troop went to the Church at Norton Hall. A very good service taken by the Rector the Rev Smith.

 

 

 

11

A very wet day and vehicles are already getting bogged which is a bad sign. Courses continued throughout the morning. A signal exercise “Awake” was held from 0430-0800.

 

 

 

12

Bty technical week continued throughout the morning. The AAQMG visited the Bty during the afternoon to discuss hutting arrangements. A wet afternoon.

 

 

 

13

A very wet and unpleasant day. Training again during the morning at BHQ. Tents are beginning to leak and men’s kits are getting damp. Some tents will have to be vacated, the dining room used as temporary quarters till the weather clears.

 

 

 

14

Petrol-less day so no officers morning was held, except for laying tests held at Don Troop position. Our 3 officer umpires for “Duck” exercise to be held this week end were passed out by the CO on miniature range. Weather much better today & beginning to dry up nicely. Also some ballast has been fetched for the worst of the potholes.

 

 

 

15

A busy and very wet day; rain being almost continuous. Stores were loaded throughout the day for exercise “Duck” which was starting early on Saturday morning. The officers were tested in miniature range by the CO at Wickford, after which was the usual BCs conference. The Battery in the mean time were preparing for their anti-tank shoot at Foulness. The column formed up at BHQ and were taken to the ranges by Capt Robertson. Only 2 detachments were taken from each troop and all fired one gun which was brought by Don Troop. Tea was had before firing at 1800 hrs. All detachments had trials with the Bren attachment using tracer before the 25 Pdr ammo was used. Sgt Huxtable’s detachment from Charlie Troop was congratulated by the AIG for scoring 5 hits out of 7 shots. Each detachment then had 4 rounds of 25 pdr ammo charge III. Satisfactory results were obtained in most cases. The anti-tank rifle was fired afterwards, but the visibility was by then getting bad. It was late (2100 hrs) by the time BHQ was reached on the return journey.

 

 

Exercise Duck

16

“Duck” started propitiously as regards weather it being brilliantly fine after the downpour of the previous day. The Battery was ordered to be in action in a previously recced position by 0930, but the movement table showed that we had to pass the start point at 0630 so a very early start had to be made. The battery was well established early on with one OP (line comm) & one more or less in the same spot with W/L. During the morning a regtl observed concentration was fired by Capt Robertson all Batteries being linked by RCS line. “Paratroops” attacked C Tp at 1115 but were all mopped up quickly. The Battery OP moved to Maldon about 1300 hrs, where it was established in the tower of the Town Hall. Barrage tracings were received for an attack timed to go in at 1730 hrs. Barrage finished at 1800 hrs and Bty was then ordered to move forward a distance of some 1500x. Great difficulty was experienced in finding a position owing to the Regt’s area being very limited and the country being almost exclusively arable. However at 2050 both troops were ready for action. Defensive fire tasks were worked out but not required during the night.

 

 

 

17

Breakfast was eaten at 0500 hrs and the Battery was on the move by 0545. A long delay at the start point before orders were received from the CO. However the battery was in action on Tiptree Heath by 0845 and an observed concentration was brought down by 0900 hrs. The MGRA visited the position shortly afterwards and stayed for 20 minutes. At 1100 hrs the battery was on the move again and took up a position at Little Tey. A concentration was fired by C Tp from the position at 1200 hrs. At 1400 orders were received to move forward again. This time about 2000x in a N/E direction. Battery position near Satehouse Farm.  OP established in a house in Earl’s Colne. A barrage tracing was received at 1930 hrs and completed 1½ hrs later. Ammo was due to arrive at Troop positions from Div Ammo Coy shortly after 2000 hrs but difficulty was experienced as lorries got lost & one broke down. Don troop received their ammo about 2300 hrs but Charlie Troop only got theirs at 0445 hrs the following morning.

 

 

 

18

The barrage was duly fired at 0500 in steady rain and on its conclusion at 0530 hrs the exercise finished. Breakfast was then eaten and the return march begun at 0830: BHQ being reached about 1000 hrs. Major MacMillan returned from leave.

 

 

 

19

Found necessary to move vehicles of BHQ to Cold Norton station where there are hard standings. Their own field having become quite impossible due to mud.

 

 

 

20

Weather much improved. The clay on which we live dries up almost as quickly as it becomes waterlogged.

 

 

 

21

Weather continues to hold up. Petrol-less day rather restricts activities. Bty HQ went for a route march of about 8-9 miles in the afternoon, 30 min of which was done in respirators.

 

 

 

22

Moved D Tp vehicles to join BHQ at Cold Norton station: a bit crowded but good standings are worth the risk. Visited by Brig Mitchelmore comd 134 Bde Gp who approved parking of vehicles in station also decided that ammunition with D Tp surplus to 1st line, should be held by RHQ as it would not be possible to move it all in the event of D Tp being called on to carry out mobile role. Dance held in BHQ dining hall in evening. Great success.

 

 

 

23

Rained hard all day. Nothing of importance to record.

 

 

 

24

BHQ & D Tp attended service at Cold Norton Church. Bde sports in which we were supposed to be competing cancelled owing to ground being unsuitable. Weather slightly improved.

 

 

 

25

Bty HQ and D Tp took part in exercise Dettingen with 8 Devon to practice defence of Latchingdon Gap. Bty also provided an OP to observe the fire of 383 Bty who fire on the gap from South of R. Crouch. Enemy driven off with heavy loss.

 

 

 

26

D Tp concentrated most of their energies on preparing new A/Tk range found on banks of R Crouch near Burnham. Target has been constructed on old car chassis and is towed by quads. Chief difficulty was provision of suitable cable to tow it, but eventually we obtained some old balloon cable from Air Ministry, which served admirably.

 

 

 

27

Major General R de H. Morgan Comd 45 Div came to see our new A/Tk range in action and appeared to be well satisfied.

 

 

 

28

Petrol-less day. Most of the day spent in preparing for journey to Larkhill on following day. Bty HQ went for their weekly route march and got thoroughly soaked by a heavy shower.

 

 

 

29

Battery carried out first stage of journey to Larkhill spending the night at a staging camp at Streatley near our former home at Basildon. Move was carried out in Regtl column. London traversed by North Circular Road under guidance of Metropolitan Police. One quad (D Troop’s ) broke down with clutch trouble and had to be removed by LAD.

 

 

 

30

Journey continued to Larkhill via Newbury and Andover. Lovely weather. Arrived shortly after midday at Fargo Camp.

 

 

Larkhill

31

Firing on Larkhill ranges. Regt fired the opening line of a standard barrage, a regimental concentration and a number of observed shoots. Capt Robertson and Chamberlain and Lt Head did shoots. Standard of shooting at OP greatly improved. Guns shooting well.
