War Diary

 

384th Field Battery RA 142nd Field Regiment RA

 

PLACE

DATE

 

 

Playden

1 Nov 1940

The advance party of the 257 Battery arrived at Rye under Major Shrimpton. MC troops were busy packing baggage. A wet day.

 

CC

 

2

Went round all the Troop positions command posts and OPs with Major Shrimpton and explained the lay out. NCOs in charge of Signallers went over the lines. Fair day.

 

CC

 

3

Went round with Major Shrimpton to OPs and handed over all traces maps and Operation orders. Troops continued packing and cooking pasties etc for the meal for the rail party which go on Nov 4th. A wet day.

 

CC

 

4

The guns of the receiving Battery arrived at 0200 hrs in the morning having been eight hours late. The guns were received by sections and the hand over went very smoothly. The main rail party arrived at 0500 hrs and marched to hides while the 384 Fd Bty had breakfast. At 0900 hrs the receiving Battery took over the guns and the 384 Fd Bty moved into a hide “The Hooks” at Playden until entraining at 2000 hrs. The main road party proceeded and billeted at Uckfield that night. A very wet day, which cleared towards evening.

 

 

Tickhill

5

The rail party spent the day in the train. The road party left Uckfield at 0745 hrs and arrived at Pangbourne about 1500 hrs where they billeted for the night. A fine day.

 

 

 

6

The road party left Pangbourne at 0745 and billeted at Lutterworth where they arrived at about 1500 hrs. A very wet day, but the men had good opportunities of drying clothes. The food was good at this staging camp.

 

 

 

7

The road party left Lutterworth at 0745 hrs and arrived at Bawtry at about 1430 hrs. Vehicles were dropped off at X Bty and 383 Bty and the men proceeded to their Billets.

 

 

 

8

The Battery spent all day getting straight and sorting out stores. The yards around the billets needed a good deal of cleaning. A wet day.

 

 

 

9

Maintenance & inspections in the morning. Showers.

 

 

 

10

Church Parade at the Parish Church Tickhill at 1100 hrs. A very wet day.

 

 

 

11

Troop training in the morning and afternoon. The signallers and Acs had a separate course. A fatigue party was carting rubble for vehicle standings at Styrrup. A fair day.

 

 

 

12

Troop training in the morning and afternoon. Signallers & Acs course. A wet day.

 

 

 

13

The Battery was inspected by the Divisional Commander in the morning. Troop training in the morning and afternoon. A fine day.

 

 

 

14

Troop training in the morning and afternoon. Signallers and Acs course. A night occupation was held at Styrrup which was successful. The guns were unloaded from the Portees without trouble.  A fine day. Lt Barnaby left for a Signal course.

 

 

 

15

Troop training in the morning. The Commanding Officer inspected the Motor Transport. A fine day.

 

 

 

16

Maintenance, Stables and inspections in the morning. The Battery played Harworth Colliery in the afternoon. A fine day.

 

 

 

17

Church parade at Tickhill at 1100 hrs. A very wet day.

 

 

 

18

Troop training in the morning and afternoon. Signallers and Acs course. A fine day.

 

 

 

18

It was with great regret that the Battery heard of the sudden death of driver Sidney Frayne, from meningitis & pneumonia in Doncaster Infirmary. Enlisting before the war he was a regular attender of drills at South Molton, often bicycling the dozen odd miles from his home at East Anstey to the drill hall. After embodiment he was attached to 384th Battery and Freddie Troop in particular, until September when he was sent to RHQ. Always cheerful, bright and willing he will be sadly missed by his many friends in the Battery and Regiment. His father and sister were at his bedside at the end, and the coffin was sent to East Anstey for burial; the internment taking place on Saturday Nov 23rd. A handsome wreath from the officers & men of the Royal Devon Yeomanry was sent and BSM Richards (on sick leave in Barnstaple) attended the funeral as representative of the Regiment.

 

ADS

 

19

D & E Troops had a troop drill order in the area Sandbeck Park. Two positions were occupied and the second on very quickly. The French 75 were quickly unloaded from the Portees.

 

 

 

20

Major Chichester left for 7 days leave. Lt Roland Jones left for a boxing course of 10 days at York. A wet morning, during which the gunners had indoor instruction, and got ready for the COs inspection.

 

 

 

1400 hrs

COs inspection of the whole Battery. Though several men had duty battle dress (which was not their fault, as recent indents have not come through) on the whole the CO was satisfied.

 

ADS

 

1630 hrs

Divisional barrage received at the Battery, and worked out by the CPO & his staff and the 2 troops. Completed in good time just over 3 hrs.

 

 

 

21

Most of the Battery went out on a practice movement with the forward Body of a Battalion. Did not prove very much except for the size of such a body, which is considerable. Gunners went to Styrrup PM and 1800 hrs practiced night occupation.

 

ADS

 

22

Another practice for most of the Battery in the “gaining of Contact Battle”. Edward Troop came into action very quickly and were complimented by the CO. Unfortunately we now hear that Ben Troop is to be given the role in the demonstration as they have 4.5 hours. The AIG spent the day with the Battery, during the morning lecturing to the Surveyors, while in the afternoon he took the gunners in gun drill at Styrrup. Baths & Pay after tea completed the day. Fine.

 

ADS

 

23

A day of fatigues and inspections. A very thorough Stables being held at 1200 hrs. The Battery Soccer XI beat a reserve team from the colliery during the afternoon. A fine day.

 

 

 

24

Church Parade at Tickhill – Preacher the Rev Meek. Another fine day.

 

ADS

 

25

Battery drill order in the Barnby Moor area. Considering the fact that the Battery had not done a drill order for over 6 months, it wasn’t too bad. Gunners were good but Signallers (especially) and Acks want more practice and generally waking up. Battery advanced by troops. Returned to Tickhill by 1600 hrs. The AIG was out & gave useful criticism at the end of the day. Fine.

 

ADS

 

26

Gunners went to Styrrup for the morning, doing camouflage nets and gun drill. Afternoon parades at Tickhill included Rifle & marching drills. PT and baths.

 

ADS

 

27

Drill order again. Nearly all the same personnel as went out on Monday parading again. District North of Bawtry chosen for 1st position. Things appeared to go smoother, and signallers especially got going quicker and the whole show had more life. Withdrew to a defensive position near Gringley-in-the-Hill. Returned to Tickhill by 1530 hrs. Conference at RHQ on the exercise to be held on the following day at 1730 hrs.

 

ADS

 

28

134th Inf Bde Exercise, 142nd Fd Regt recce parties took part in this exercise. Formed up at 0930 hrs, but column did not move off until 1045 hrs. We then proceeded to an unknown destination to take up a defensive position on the river Aire. Quite a long trek and vehicles must have done about 100 miles during the day. The Artillery appeared to be in action very quickly after the giving out of orders, and by 1300 hrs could have brought down fire. Unfortunately the remainder of the day was rather wasted from our point of view, as the Infantry took till nearly 1600 hrs reconnoitring their positions. Left the start point for the return at 1711 hrs, and did not get back to Tickhill till 1840 hrs, so it was almost completely a night drive. An interesting day taken all round, but seemed to be painfully slow during the afternoon. Major Chichester returned from leave. Concert party from Doncaster entertained the Troops at the Miner’s Institute during the evening.

 

ADS

 

29

The Battery Recce party held a Brigade Signal exercise all day which was successfully carried out. There was a Muster Parade in the morning to condemn battle dress. Individual training later in the morning. A fine day.

 

ADS

 

30

Inspections and stables in the morning.
