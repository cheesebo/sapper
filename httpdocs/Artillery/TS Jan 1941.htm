War Diary

 

384th Field Battery RA 142nd Field Regiment RA

 

PLACE

DATE

 

 

Tickhill

1 Jan 1941

Troop training in the morning. Officers and NCOs course in the afternoon. A fine day.

 

CC

 

2

Battery shot on the open range near Sheffield. Officers & NCOs course in the afternoon. A fine day.

 

CC

 

3

The Battery was inspected by the CRA both at Rock House and Styrrup. Courses and troop training.

 

CC

 

4

Inspections maintenance in the morning. Half holiday in the afternoon. A fine day.

 

CC

 

5

Church Parade at Tickhill Parish Church. A fine day.

 

CC

 

6

Troop training in the morning and afternoon. Officers and NCOs instruction in the afternoon.

 

CC

 

7

Troop training in the morning and afternoon. Instruction for officers and NCOs in the afternoon. A fine day.

 

CC

 

8

A TEWT was held for Officers and NCOs to practise the Regiment coming into action. A fine day.

 

CC

 

9

The whole Battery had their respirators tested in the morning. Officers and NCOs gun drill competition in the afternoon. 384 Bty was first with the NCOs team and first with the Officers team.

 

CC

 

10

Troop training in morning and afternoon. Officers & NCOs instruction in afternoon. A fine day.

 

CC

 

11

Stables and maintenance in the morning. Half holiday. A fine day.

 

CC

 

12

Church Parade at Bircotes at 1015 hrs. The regiment was inspected by the CO. A fine day.

 

CC

 

13

Troop training in the morning and afternoon. 2 25 pdrs were collected from the Quartermaster for practice, before we go to camp. A fine day.

 

CC

 

14

Troop training in the morning and afternoon. A fine day.

 

CC

 

15

Troop training in the morning and afternoon. Signallers concentrated on rifle drill. A fine day.

 

CC

 

16

The Battery was inspected while doing gun drill by the CRA. Gun drill and rifle drill in the afternoon. A fine cold day. Advance party left for Grassington.

 

CC

 

17

The Battery spent most of the day loading stores and preparing for the move to Grassington for the weeks shooting. A fine day.

 

CC

Grassington

18

The Battery moved by road and rail for Grassington. The road party left at 1000 hrs arriving at destination about 1615 hrs. However several vehicles were late owing to heavy fall of snow. One ammunition vehicle was completely snowed up and it is doubtful if it will be able to get in for several days. All the other vehicles eventually got in but very late.

The billets are not very good and some are rather crowded, but as the weather is so cold it doesnt matter much. There is a good room for the canteen at the cinema and the men should be comfortable there. It is snowing hard late at night and doubtful if we shall be able to shoot for several days.

 

CC

 

19

The battery moved to Grassington from Tickhill by road and rail. The rail party left at 1015 hrs and the road party at 1000 hrs. All went smoothly until Blubberhouses was reached when snow began to fall and vehicles began to stick on the hill. All the Battery vehicles except one managed to get in in the end. Billets at Grassington are reasonably comfortable. Cold day & snow.

 

CC

 

20

Spent all day rescuing vehicles from the snow drifts and digging snow from the gun park. Snow nearly all day.

 

CC

 

21

Digging snowdrifts all day on the Skipton road, Rylstone was reached a point about way. Very cold with fine snow in the afternoon.

 

CC

 

22

Rations are running short and we have only days rations before we go onto hard rations. Digging parties digging away snow drifts on the Skipton Road until 1400 hrs when the town was reached, and rations were collected from the station. Cold and snow all day turning to sleet & rain in the evening. The thaw started.

 

CC

 

23

Battery spent all day digging on the Green How Hill Road and got to within a mile of the Hill Top. Cold but bright day.

 

CC

 

24

Battery spent day digging and the signallers received wireless instruction from Lt Barnaby. Cold day.

 

CC

 

25

Battery was told to be prepared to shoot, and arrived at the gun position but the fog was too thick to be able to observe so the shooting was cancelled. Snow in the evening.

 

CC

 

26

383 and X Fd Bty shot on the ranges and 384 Bty Officers watched the shooting. The remainder of the Battery dug on the Bolton Abbey road. Slight fog on the Hill.

 

CC

 

27

The Battery prepared to shoot but the fog was too thick and the shooting was cancelled. The Battery afterwards went out to dig snowdrifts on the Bolton Road. Thick fog and snow.

 

CC

 

28

The Battery proceeded via Bolton Abbey to the ranges. The visibility was very poor and only four rounds were fired which were unobserved. Cold with sleet falling all day.

 

CC

 

29

The Battery was prepared to shoot but owing to poor visibility the shooting was cancelled. The battery dug on the Blubberhouses Road. A dance was held in the cinema which was a great success and thoroughly enjoyed by all. Foggy day.

 

CC

 

30

Shooting was again cancelled owing to fog. In the morning a gas demonstration was carried out by 383 Fd Bty. Miniature range and a lecture by the IGs on air burst ranging and smoke screens in the afternoon. Foggy & cold.

 

CC

 

31

Shooting was again cancelled owing to fog and snow. Maintenance and gun drill for remainder of day. Miniature range for all officers and A/Cs in the afternoon. Snow and fog.
