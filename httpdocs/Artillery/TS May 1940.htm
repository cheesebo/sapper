War Diary

 

384th Field Battery RA 142nd Field Regiment RA

 

PLACE

DATE

 

 

Okehampton

May 1 1940

Bty without guns. Training proceeded according to programme. F Troop still away on Billeting exercise. Organised games for remainder of Bty held in the afternoon. Night driving exercise by troops. New class of signallers started training. CO inspected Bty at 1130 hrs.

 

PC

 

2

D Troop on duty. F troop returned from billeting exercise at 1315 hrs. Drill order by E Troop. Three positions were occupied with increasing speed. A tank shoot was improvised using motor cyclists as the targets.

 

PC

 

3

Training as per programme. All officers attended a TEWT run by the two Bty Commanders. Major Chichester left for Senior Officer’s course & Lt Boileau took over temporary command of the Bty.

 

PC

 

4

Bty went on 8 mile route march, respirators being worn at intervals. Afternoon half holiday.

 

PC

 

5

CO inspected the Regiment at Church Parade. After Church recreation. Weather fine.

 

PC

 

6

D Troop drill order on moor with IG. F Troop employed on filling in the gun pits dug during the previous months. F Troop did a successful night operation on the Moor.

 

PC

 

7

Training proceeded as per programme. RHQ staff and BCs went on a TEWT arranged for the Div Arty by the BRA. Subject Attack. E & F Troops had a combined drill order. Two positions were occupied and move carried out by leap-frogging. D troop had a night occupation on the moor.

 

PC

 

8

Continuation of BRAs TEWT. Subject Withdrawal. F Troop had drill order on moor and practised air shoots with O procedure. Concert was given in the evening for the troops by a party from Bideford.

 

PC

 

9

Training proceeded according to programme. Laying tests and construction of gun pit camouflages. Bty staff practised Stage IV and remainder outdoor silent registration. Weather fine.

 

PC

 

10

Invasion of Low Countries by Germany became known by Bty at 0700 hrs. Training proceeded normally and fatigues were occupied with the approaching move to Hatherleigh. Drill order by E Troop on moor. On return, orders were received for an immediate move to an unknown destination. Packing & checking of stores & equipment continued until a late hour. All leave cancelled & men away recalled.

 

PC

 

11

Orders were received to strike the camp at Hatherleigh & return materials to Devonport. Packing continued all day, but no further orders about the move were received. Regtl advance party left at about 1200 hrs.

 

PC

 

12

Church Parade was held as usual, Muster parade of Bty at 1400 hrs. Equipment loaded on trucks for conveyance to station by 1600 hrs. Parties began to load train at 1800 hrs. Final parade at 2130 hrs for march to station. This was led by Town Band & most of the inhabitants of Okehampton had turned out to bid the troop farewell. The first train left at 0015 hrs.

 

PC

En route & Warbleton

13

Journey uneventful. Arrived Heathfield, Sussex 0915 hrs. Party was met by 2nd I/C & Bty was marched to its billets led by guides. Work was at once started on organising the Billets & continued till dark. Billets were private houses with the exception of D Troop which had Army huts. Bty HQ & HQ Troop established at Little Horham House Warbleton.

 

PC

 

14

Work was continued on billets & general organisation. Conference held at RHQ & officers were warned of the likelihood of an early invasion of England. Sites for Road Blocks were reconnoitred. Lt Robertson returned to duty after a month’s attachment with the BEF.

 

PC

 

15

At 0145 hrs the following order as per appendix A attached was received. Stand down was received at 0500 hrs. Conference at Bde HQ at about 1200 hrs allotted to Bty 3 road blocks, ½ of block to be in position by dusk, material for other ½ to be ready. This was completed. A mini rifle range was constructed at E Troop billet, and as many of the Bty as possible fired.

 

PC

 

16

Training according to programme, road blocks being constructed, & as much firing on miniature rifle range carried out as possible.

 

PC

 

17

Training as per programme. Additional rifles were delivered to the Bty, also 50 Rds .303 SAA per rifle. E Troop moved to new quarters at Horham Manor which is adjacent to their road block, which is 4 miles from Bty HQ.

 

PC

 

18

Troop Commanders arranged drills, consisting mainly of Mini Rifle Range & Block construction. In afternoon inter Troop Cricket match was held.

 

PC

 

19

Church Parade for HQ & F Troops at Warbleton Church. E Troop Horham Church, D Troop at RHQ. Afternoon spent in further block construction.

 

PC

 

20

One Bren gun was delivered to each troop, & instruction on its use & mechanism was given to the Troops. Bren gun detachments were formed. HQ & F Troops carried out a practice Road block occupation at 0000 hrs. Weather fine.

 

PC

 

21

Further road block construction. E Troop carried out practice road block occupation at 0000 hrs. Great aerial activity this night.

 

PC

 

22

Training as per programme. D Troop Road Block exercise at 0000 hrs.

 

PC

 

23

Training as per programme. Order received that by day only one sentry was to guard each road block.

PC

 

24

Training as per programme. F Troop did gun drill on 384 Bty guns.

 

PC

 

25

F Troop were ordered to hand over their road block to HQ Troop & from now on to become a mobile platoon to deal with parachutists. They are under 134 Bde orders who are also providing transport. F Troop held practise manoeuvre in the afternoon. Subject, dealing with supposed parachute landing in the vicinity.

 

PC

 

26

At 0125 hrs the order came through – Man all road blocks immediately – Stand down was ordered at 0245 hrs. Troops back in billets by 0330 hrs. At 0730 hrs Parties were detailed to remove road blocks constructed early this morning. Church Parade as on 19 May 1940.

 

PC

 

27

Regulation GS Gas capes were issued to the Bty. Drill order by HQ & E Tps with 383 Bty’s 18 Pdrs, at Stone House Park. 2/Lt Hyett had motor bicycle accident which proved fatal. He died in hospital at about 0000 hrs following an internal operation. A sad loss to the Bty.

 

PC

 

28

Further work on block construction & sites for weapon pits. A court of inquiry held at BHQ on 2/Lt Hyett’s death.

 

PC

 

29

Construction on road blocks continue, particularly for all-round defence. F Troop made a practice attack on Bde HQ to test latter’s defences against parachutists. Weather thundery & very wet. Order received as per Appendix B attached. Practice ammo for Bren guns received & HQ Troop fired.

 

PC

 

30

Construction of road blocks continued as the order was received that they are now to be tank proof. Training as per programme in afternoon & Bren gun shooting by D E & F troops.

 

PC

 

31

At 0055 hrs orders as per Appendix C were received. Complete road blocks were constructed & fully manned. F Troop stood to awaiting instructions from Bde. At 0900 hrs orders were received that the Bty was at 4 hrs notice by day & ½ hr notice by night. Order to rest troops as much as possible also received, & further work on blocks done in shifts. F Troop providing some of these. At 1700 hrs 4 25/18 pdr Mark IV guns & Limbers were delivered to F Troop less equipment. This troop also received 3 3Ton Lorries & 6 drivers from 6 Bde. These are to remain with F troop until further notice. Weather fine.

PC

 

 

 

 

 

 

Appendix “A”

Order received by 2nd Lt. FEG Graham from the Adjutant, 142nd Field Regiment, at 0145 Hours, 15.5.40 as follows:-

“Information has been received from a reliable source that an invasion is contemplated by the enemy this morning at 04.00 hours. All troops will stand to at that time until 05.00 hours. Road Block positions will be manned but no blocking done until further orders”.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Appendix “B”

To: Both Batteries

From: Adjutant, 142nd Field Regiment RA

Particular vigilance against sea or air landings to-day & to-morrow is ordered as a result of an intercepted message.

29.5.40

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Appendix “C”

To: 384th Field Battery RA

From: RHQ

Attack by Air and fast Boats carrying 100 men considered imminent.. Man Coast Defences immediately.. Road blocks and Mobile Reserve at 15 minutes notice.. accelerate work on defences from daylight.

31.5.40
