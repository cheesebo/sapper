War Diary

 

384th Field Battery RA 142nd Field Regiment RA

 

PLACE

DATE

 

 

Didlington

1 June 1941

Sunday – and Church parade in the camp.

 

 

 

2

Bty training. 17 layers were passed this pm

 

 

 

3

Move back to Basildon – all went smoothly, and the journey was completed in the day – much to everyone’s satisfaction.

 

 

 

4

Maintenance and inspection of vehicles following the move. The Bty seemed glad to be back after its wanderings in Norfolk.

 

FGMM

 

5

Umpires demonstration, attended by all officers and Nos 1 Duke of Gloucester was present and officers were presented to him. Major C Chichester relinquished command of the Bty today, and Capt F G Macmillan took over.

 

 

 

6

Training under Tp commanders

 

 

 

7

Capt Macmillan instituted a more rigorous form of inspection at Sat. Stables than has been done before.

 

 

 

8

Sunday – GOs Church parade and half holiday in afternoon.

 

 

 

9

A divisional whole holiday in lieu of the one the regiment missed on Whitmonday.

 

 

 

10

Training under Tp commanders

 

 

 

11

Today a Brigade exercise started in the afternoon and Regt moved off to lying up area near Easthampstead Park, where it passed a night which was slightly disturbed by “parachutists”.

 

 

 

12

Contact was gained with the enemy during the morning on the line of the river Loden and the battle waged all day, with some loss to RHQ, which was overrun by enemy carriers. The Bty settled in well for the night, which was unfortunately disturbed by the order to stand to about 0600 hrs, on account of more parachutists.

 

 

 

13

Came back to Basildon today and afternoon was spent on maintenance

 

 

 

14

Stables and half holiday in p.m.

 

 

 

15

No church parade today. Morning was spent in maintenance. Half holiday in p.m.

 

 

 

16

A/Tank shooting on new range with Bren attachment, Miniature range for officers.

 

 

 

17

Bty staff drill order, which went well. Lovely weather.

 

 

 

18

Training under Tp Commanders.

 

FGMM

Basildon

19           0800

Bulldog exercise officially started at 0800 hrs when Battery Billeting parties under the 2nd I/C left Basildon House to recce a lying up area for the Regt at North Mymms Park

 

 

0915

The Regt left the gates at Basildon at 0915 and had an uneventful trip to the DP where they were met by Bty representatives at 1500 hrs.

 

 

1500

The lying up area was a good one there being plenty of cover and space.

 

 

1730

The Billeting parties were again on the road at 17.30 hrs to select a further lying up area near Great Canfield.

 

 

2322

 

The Regt was passed the SP for this further move at 23.22 hrs and reached the DP about 0200 hrs, the column moving rather too fast a pace in the dark.

ADS

 

20           0200

Unfortunately DRVR Northcott who was acting as a DR met with an accident on the way from the DP to the lying up area and had to be taken to hospital in Bishop STORTFORD suffering from concussion.

 

 

Great Canfield

0400

Again quite a good area had been located at Marsh’s Farm and the Regt was settled in at 0400 hrs. Breakfast was followed by maintenance and a talk by troop commanders on the objects of the exercise, which in brief was to push across the frontier of Norland and capture Norwich. Another hot and cloudless day and everybody got plenty of rest during the afternoon. After tea there was a gas drill practice for all ranks.

 

 

1730

Billeting parties again went forward at 1730, and the Regt moved out about 2200 hrs. The BC in the meantime had gone forward with the Battalion Commander 6th Devons to reconnoitre the frontier for the attack which had been planned for 0430 on the 22nd. This journey was uneventful but again the column moved too fast especially as no lights were allowed for the latter part of the journey. The Quad drivers were particularly good in negotiating the dark narrow lanes at such a speed and without an accident.

ADS

 

21          0230

The DP was reached about 0230 and everybody was settled in at the new lying up area by 0400. An all round defence was thoroughly prepared as parachute attacks were expected at any time. No incident occurred though and the day passed peacefully in very pleasant surroundings with a very kind host in the local farmer.

 

 

1800

The BC gave out orders at 1800 hrs and the Battery was put under command 6th Devons, and ordered to go into action covering bridges over the River Waveney where the attack was to go in at dawn on the 22nd.

ADS

 

22          0045

The Battery arrived at its RV about 0045 hrs and were quickly in action. Tasks were worked out and Don Troop were ordered to form part of the Forward Body in the attack. Actually no enemy were encountered in this area except for a few Home Guards, and the gunners support was not required.

 

 

 

0430

Don Troop left shortly after dawn; Charlie Troop and BHQ remaining in action till the main body had deployed and when marching up the axis of advance. Another perfect day and everybody was getting sunburnt and looking much more healthy than when they started. A rather dull morning ensued, the Battery following the marching infantry in short bounds. The monotony was only relieved by the news that Germany had started attacking Russia, and speculation on this new aspect in the war was rife – On the march the CPO had orders to go forward to meet the 2nd I/C just short of Norwich, and on the way, his car was ambushed by a cleverly concealed Home Guard strong point and he himself was severely “wounded” and put out of action for the remainder of the exercise. There was rather confusion after this in the Battery, and both Don and Charlie Troops neatly walked into an ambush again by Home guards. Don Troop having their TPSM and 1 gun put out of action, while Charlie Troop were more fortunate and succeeded in turning round guns and vehicles in a narrow lane very quickly.

 

 

1630

Charlie Troop went into action for about 5 minutes only. Still no support having been required from the Battery as only local Home Guards were resisting. Incidentally those Home guards were proving their worth in no uncertain manner, and the Battery casualties from ambushes up to this stage were considerable: One officer and 8 OPs “wounded” 4 ORs “missing” and 3 vehicles out of action as well as a quad & gun & 2 MCs. 136th Inf Bde were then apparently taken away from the main attack on Norwich and ordered to hold a defensive line to the South West. The Battery moved to the new area about 1900 hrs and the position occupied successfully shortly after 2200 hrs. The Ops during the forward body move had consisted of Charlie Tp Cmdr going forward with the Div Recce Btn and Don Tp Cmdr with the forward body. Unfortunately TOC truck with the recce Btn had been knocked out early in the proceeding, the TP cmdr escaping and getting into another vehicle. However the range for the wireless was too great and no information at all was received during the day from this source. In the new position 2 OPs went out while the Battery was coming into action.

ADS

 

23

The following morning contact still had not been gained though the mobile OP was sending back useful information.

 

 

1000

The first rounds fired by the Battery went off at about 1000 hrs being concentrations on various points causing the enemy to refire. Unfortunately just as the battle was getting interesting 136 Inf Bde was ordered to retire and take up a defensive position behind the river Waveney – 384 Bty leap frogging troops back in support of the rear guard. Don troop left to take up an intermediary position behind the Rly line, Charlie Troop being in action south of Attleborough.

 

 

2015

Orders for Charlie Troop to move back came at 2015 and they proceeded along the axis of withdrawal to the new area at Abbey farm. Unfortunately bridges over the river had been prematurely blown, and traffic was being diverted. A scene of indescribable confusion with Brigades moving in opposite directions near the river occurred, but C Tp and BHQ managed to get through successfully, and take a detour of some 20 miles to get over the river.

 

 

2300

Another hair raising journey ensued, but the Troop  BHQ  arrived intact at its destination about 2300 hrs instead of 2130 owing to the long detour.

ADS

 

24

Don Troop arrived shortly after midnight and Ops went out ready to find positions at first light. No more shooting was done however by the Battery, and the exercise officially ended at 1130 hrs. The return march was started still in Battalion groups at 1730 hrs. Unfortunately on the way to the start point a wrong turning was taken and this meant allowing the 9th Devons through the column while the Battery caught up. The DP was at Sudbury, and we were led from there to a lying up area for the night.

 

ADS

­

25

A late start the following morning for the final trek home – about 120 miles and Basildon was successfully reached about 2015 hrs, after a very hot all day drive.

 

ADS

Basildon

26

Today was spent organising things after our week’s absence. There was plenty to do.

 

FGMM

 

27

Talk on Bulldog exercise. Training under Tp arrangements. The lovely weather still holds, and swimming is being organised for the men.

 

FGMM

 

28

Stables and some A/Tank shooting, which is going quite well. It is a pity a rather longer range is not available.

 

FGMM

 

29

COs Church parade and half holiday in p.m.

 

FGMM

 

30

Regiment moved off to Larkhill for 1 days shooting. We bivouacked at Fargo Camp. Lovely warm weather with heavy dew at night.
