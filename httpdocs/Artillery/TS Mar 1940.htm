War Diary

 

384th Field Battery RA 142nd (RDY) Field Regiment RA

 

1940

Friday

March 1st

PT 0800 hrs. Marching Drill 09.30 hrs. Troop Training remainder of morning. Pay Parade & PT in the afternoon.

 

ADS

Sat

March 2nd

 

PT 0800 hrs. Muster Parade and Medical Inspection of whole Battery. Billet Inspection. All men found to be well turned out on parade.

 

ADS

Sun

March 3rd

Church Parade 0930 hrs. Preacher Rev. Compton.  A fine day. Mr Roland Jones a new officer appointed to the Battery – Temporarily posted to Edward Troop.

 

ADS

Mon

March 4th

PT 0715 hrs. Marching Drill – 0915 hrs. Troop Training & written examinations remainder of morning. CO & Capt. Keene inspected Bty Training – 1330 hrs. Bath Parade & vaccination by MO.

 

ADS

Tues

March 5th

PT 0715 hrs. March to Camp. Laying Tests – 5 passed. Bty Staff Digging 1330 hrs. Gun Drill & Digging etc. A fine day. Lt. Williams reported for duty after gunnery course at Larkhill.

 

ADS

Wed

March 6th

M Parry, new Officer reported for duty & temporarily posted to Don Troop.  384th Bty Soccer team got into final of Divisional cup by defeating 5th Btn. SLI (9-1). Another lovely day.

 

ADS

Thurs

March 7th

Bty Staff Drill Order (Highhampton district) – A fine day & a useful day’s training which was favourably commented on by the CO & 2nd I/C. 2 Infantry Officers & a Signal’s Officer were out with us.

 

ADS

Fri

March 8th

PT & March to camp. Bty Staff did a miniature lay out which was a useful exercise to be repeated. Pay and PT in the afternoon.

 

ADS

Sat

March 9th

PT & gun Drill for rather a small Battery owing to leave & duty. Half holiday P.M.

 

ADS

Sun

March 10th

Church Parade 0930 hrs. Preacher Rev. Compton. Fine day.

 

ADS

Mon

March 11th

Training in the Town. Nothing to note.

 

ADS

Tues

March 12th

Bty Staff Drill order in Hatherleigh districts. Very good 1st Position but owing to delays signal communication never got through in the 2nd position which spoiled an otherwise good day. Heavy rain in the afternoon didn’t help.

 

ADS

Wed

March 13th

Troop Training & organised games P.M. 142nd Regt defeated 96th Regt at Rugger by 9-3.

 

ADS

Thurs

March 14th

Troop Training which included a vehicle movement table & map reading exercise.

 

ADS

Frid

March 15th

Troop Training & Pay Parade.

 

ADS

Sat

March 16th

Usual daily training A.M. Half Holiday P.M.

 

ADS

Sun

March 17th

Church Parade attended by the Brigadier General Anderson who read the lesson & took the salute at the march past.

 

ADS

Mon

March 18th

Usual daily training in the town.

 

ADS

Tues

March 19th

Usual daily training in the camp. Our first night route march began at 2100 hrs to Jacobstowe via Exbourne & was a great success. Finished about 0100 hrs.

 

ADS

Wed

March 20th

Training in the town A.M. The Regimental running team competed in the final at Tavistock P.M. & were narrowly defeated into 2nd place by the 4th Devons. 96th Fd. Regt being 3rd.

 

ADS

Thurs

March 21st

Bty Staff Drill Order in Tavistock area – Met one Battery (382) of 96th Fd. Regt. & both batteries came in to action on much the same ground.

 

ADS

Frid

March 22nd

Good Friday. Church Parade A.M. Preacher Rev. Compton. Organised sports in the afternoon which were well attended & appreciated by the men.

 

ADS

Sat

March 23rd

Nearly the whole Battery busy with fatigues etc. Half Holiday P.M.

 

ADS

Easter Sun

March 24th

Church Parade A.M. followed by Communion Service – Preacher Rev. Compton.

 

ADS

Easter Mon

March 25th

Roll Call & Inspection followed by a regrading medical parade which occupied most of the morning. P.M. A boxing display etc. and organised sports by the PT Instructor Sergt Rogers.

 

ADS

Tues

March 26th

Battery Staff Drill Order in Bridestowe district. Remainder up at the camp. A lovely day. 2/Lt Graham left for a 3 week’s CPO’s course at Larkhill.

 

ADS

Wed

March 27th

A red letter day for the Battery as we won the final of the Inter-Divisional Soccer Competition against the 5th DCLI at Plymouth by 6-4.

 

ADS

Thurs

March 28th

Laying tests all day up at the camp. 11 layers passed during the day. Afternoon Don Troop went digging. Our last day as a Battery at the camp. Major Spedding a new officer arrived, having been posted to this unit.

 

ADS

Frid

March 29th

As we were duty Battery most of the morning was taken up with getting the guns down from the camp & getting the camp cleaned up. Pay & Bath Parades P.M.  Capt Chamberlain went for ½ day’s course on Cutching & Painting of tents. Capt Chamberlain & Lt Johnson went on leave before going to Llandridnod Wells for a 2 months gunnery course.

 

ADS

Sat

March 30th

Route March for the Battery of about 5 ½ miles – Half holiday P.M. Capt AD Stoddart went on his week’s Easter leave, after duty.

 

ADS

Sun

March 31st

Church Parade at 0940 hrs at Parish Church.
