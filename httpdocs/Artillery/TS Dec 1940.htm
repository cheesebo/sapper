War Diary

 

384th Field Battery RA 142nd Field Regiment RA

 

PLACE

DATE

 

 

Tickhill

1 Dec 1940

Church Parade at Tickhill Parish Church at 1100 hrs. Drizzle all day.

 

CC

 

2

Major Mardon gave a lecture on camouflage and the A/C IG instructed the team selected for erecting camouflage. Lt Robertson was practising for the forward body demonstration. Officers of the Regiment were lectured on MT by the 384 Bt WLO Lt Lloyd and by Lt Robertson on air burst ranging.

 

CC

 

3

Forward body demonstration on the Tickhill Doncaster Road. The demonstration was successfully carried out and Lt Robertson described the action taken by the Artillery OP. Wet day.

 

CC

 

4

The Regiment was inspected by the GOC Northern Command. Lt Gen Sir Ronald Adams Baronet in the morning. There was a camouflage Competition at Bircotes in the morning. 384 Battery had the winning team. A fine day.

 

CC

 

5

Troop training in the morning and afternoon. A fine day.

 

CC

 

6

Troop training signallers and Acs class in the morning. R&O groups reconnoitred the ground for night occupation, which is to take place tomorrow morning with the CRA. The routes were marked with tape. A fine day.

 

CC

 

7

Left to occupy the position at 0300 hrs. Successfully occupied except for the fact that the 75mms were not unloaded from the portees at the rendezvous and MCs were used right up to the position. A fine day. Cleaning and maintenance was carried out on return.

 

CC

 

8

The Battery attended Regimental Church Parade at Bircotes. There was an inspection by the CO. A fine day.

 

CC

 

9

There was a battery drill order in the morning. No officers took part it being entirely conducted by NCOs. Quite a successful day. Dull weather.

 

CC

 

10

Every available man proceeded to the rifle ranges NW of Sheffield for rifle practice. Only five rounds per man was fired as the weather was so bad. Heavy showers.

 

CC

 

11

The Battery held a drill order in the morning and practised the withdrawal of a battery fighting a rearguard action. Lt Robertson and myself attended a Brigade TEWT at Gainsborough. A fine day.

 

CC

 

12

The battery practised the withdrawal. The communications worked well but the movement was rather slow. A fine day.

 

CC

 

13

A brigade moving into action exercise was held N of Doncaster. The exercise went smoothly in spite of the morning being very foggy. A fine day with a sharp frost in the morning.

 

CC

 

14

Cleaning maintenance and stables in the morning. A wet day.

 

CC

 

15

Church parade at Tickhill at 1100 hrs. A fine day.

 

CC

 

16

The withdrawal exercise was held with the CRA Brigadier de Fonblanque. The Battery withdrew by leapfrogging troops for about 25 miles. The CRA expressed himself pleased with the exercise. The communications worked well. A fine day.

 

CC

 

17

Troop training in morning and afternoon. Battery Commanders reconnoitred a shooting range near Blubberhouses NW of Doncaster. A fine day & sharp frost.

 

CC

 

18

Troop training in morning and afternoon. A regimental drivers class has been started. 12 men from each Battery are attending. A fine day. Officers had a lecture on law, orders & signalling in the afternoon.

 

CC

 

19

Troop training in morning and afternoon. Course for Acs, Signallers & drivers. A fine day.

 

CC

 

20

Troop training in morning and afternoon. Signallers, Acs & drivers course. NCOs had instruction in making appreciations, giving orders and marking maps. A fine day.

 

CC

 

21

Maintenance and inspections in the morning. In the afternoon the Battery defeated the C Coy 8th Devons 7 goals to 1. This was a Divisional Inter company match. A brilliant day.

 

CC

 

22

Church parade was held at Tickhill Parish Church at 1100 hrs. A fine day.

 

CC

 

23

Driving, Signalling and Acs training. Officers and NCOs had instruction in directors & artillery board. A fine day.

 

CC

 

24

Signallers Acs & drivers class in the morning. Gunners were helping to paint guns & vehicles which have to be painted by Jan 4th 1940 (presume 1941). A run & football was organised for the afternoon. A fine day.

 

CC

 

25

Church Parade for a service at Tickhill Parish Church. Major Mardon inspected the Battery & there were 58 men on parade. The whole Battery had their Christmas dinner at Rock House in the new dining hut. Roast Pork, Christmas pudding and mince pies were served and each man had 1 pts of beer & 10 cigarettes. A brilliant day.

 

CC

 

26

A whole holiday after a parade for roll call at 0915. A brilliant day.

 

CC

 

27

Drivers, Signallers & Acs course continued. Layers gun drill. A fine day.

 

CC

 

28

Maintenance & inspections in the morning. A fine day.

 

CC

 

29

Church parade in the morning at Tickhill Parish Church. In the afternoon the Battery played the 9th Battalion the Devonshire Right on the school football ground at Bircotes. It was a cup tie and resulted in a win for the Battery 4-0. A fine day.

 

CC

 

30

There was a muster parade for foot and boot inspection and for the MO in the morning. Officers did gun drill in the morning. In the afternoon Officers and NCOs attended lectures & instruction. One lecture was by the Brigade Major Major Bramwell-Davis on the organisation of the Infantry Brigade and battalion. A wet day.

 

CC

 

31

Drivers, signallers and Acs course in the morning. Officers and NCOs instruction in the afternoon. A dull day.

CC
