War Diary

 

384th Field Battery RA 142nd Field Regiment RA

 

PLACE

DATE

 

 

Northiam

Jul 1 1940

F Troop drill order in morning. D and E Tps shot on the open rifle range in Northiam in the morning. “Don” Tp gun drill in the afternoon. A fine day.

 

CC

 

2

Troop training in the morning and afternoon. A fine day.

 

CC

 

3

Battery Staff lay out in Northiam Park in the morning. F Tp gun drill. In the afternoon there was a demonstration for Don Force, Infantry tactics at Brede.  In the morning at 1115 the enemy dropped a bomb in Northiam Park about 300 yards from the house. It was dropped from an aeroplane flying at about 400 feet. No damage was done. L/Sgt Gullidge left for a Royal Engineers OCTU. A dull day with low clouds.

 

CC

 

4

Full Don force exercise in Peasmarsh area. A fine day.

 

CC

 

5

A “Don” force exercise was carried out against the 6th Devons in Winchelsea area. F Troop with guns took part in the exercise. Slight rain throughout the day.

 

CC

 

6

Troop Commanders inspections in the morning. Half holiday in the afternoon. A fine day. There was a cricket match between D Tp and E Tp.

 

CC

 

7

Church Parade for a Service in Brickwall Grounds at 0915. The Rev Dunbar took the service. A fine day.

 

CC

 

8

Don force exercise with guns. A pre arranged line was occupied. A fine day.

 

CC

 

9

Troop training in the morning. F Tp sight testing. A dull day.

 

CC

 

10

F Troop drill order in Brede area. Troop training in the morning and afternoon. A wet day.

 

CC

 

11

Don Force exercise. The Rye line was occupied. A wet day. A cinema show was held at Brickwall.

 

CC

 

12

F Tp drill order. A wet day.

 

CC

 

13

F Tp drill order in the morning. A wet day.

 

CC

 

14

Church Parade in Parish Church at 1100 hrs. Preacher the Rev Dunbar. The CRA after Church Parade presented medals to the football team which won the Divisional football cup. Sgt Harman was also presented with a cup as winner of the Divisional Cross Country run. A dull day. Capt. Martin & 2/Lt Graham left for a course at Larkhill.

 

CC

 

15

Don force exercise on river Rother. The enemy were represented by the 5th Loyals. A wet day. Major Howard gave a lecture on the new link shooting procedure in the evening.

 

CC

 

16

F Tp moved into a static position at Playden. D & E Tps Troop training. A dull day.

 

CC

 

17

Troop training in the morning and afternoon. Order given that all mess tins must be painted a dull khaki to prevent flash. Wet day.

 

CC

 

18

Battery staff drill order under Major Howard. Only the link line was laid, remainder of signalling was done by wireless, which worked well. A fine day.

 

CC

 

19

Troop training in the morning. Visited F Troop in the morning. Half holiday in the afternoon. A wet day.

 

CC

 

20

Troop training and inspections in the morning. Half holiday.

 

CC

 

21

Church Parade at the Parish Church Preacher the Rev Dunbar.

 

CC

 

22

Don force exercise on river Rother against 275th Anti-tank Battery. A fine day. Leave was started.

 

CC

 

23

The fortress of Brickwall was manned at 0900 hrs. Don force exercise confined to the 384th Battery. A fine day.

 

CC

 

24

Troop Training. E & HQ Troop shot on the open range at Battle. A dull day. Signallers Regimental Signal exercise.

 

CC

 

25

Troop Training. A fine day.

 

CC

 

26

A brigade wireless exercise was held. 384th Bty was in support of the 6th Devons. A fine day.

 

CC

 

27

HQ Tp shot on range at Battle. Vehicle maintenance and inspections. Wet day.

 

CC

 

28

Church Parade at the Parish Church. Preacher the Rev Dunbar. An inspection of anti-gas equipment was held after Church Parade. It was found to be in good order. A fine day.

 

CC

 

29

HQ Tp shot on range at Battle. Don Tp gun drill. E Tp Bayonet drill. A fine day.

 

CC

 

30

Don Force exercise against the 275 Anti-tank Bty. A successful day. Fine day.

 

CC

 

31

Troop Training. In the evening there was a regimental night move which was most successful. A fine day.

CC
