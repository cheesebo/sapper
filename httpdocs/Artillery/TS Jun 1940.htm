War Diary

 

384th Field Battery RA 142nd Field Regiment RA

 

PLACE

DATE

 

 

Warbleton

Jun 1 1940

Capt. Chamberlain & 2/Lt RS Johnson returned to duty after a two month course. The former took over temporary command of the Bty. Training went according to programme, except that the afternoon & evening were spent in the construction of the road blocks. Weather very fine. Further firing of Bren took place on open range.

 

PC

 

2

HQ F & D Troops held a combined Church Parade & marched to the Warbleton Parish Church. E Troop attended service at Horham Church. The afternoon was again spent in strengthening the road blocks & wire defences. Weather very fine.

 

PC

 

3

Training proceeded according to programme. 20 men were detailed to witness an 18 pdr shoot (shrapnel at Beachy head). Divisional General visited 3 Troops in the morning. Bty was ordered to send 10 men to Banstead to collect 640 Rds 25 pdr HE Amm. On arrival at F troop it was at once transferred to the limbers & amm lorries as further gun equipment had also been delivered that day. F troop was relieved of its duties as mobile parachute squad & became the first 25 pdr troop in the regt. F troop reported ready at 0130 hrs 4 Jun 1940. Weather very fine. 2nd half of road block ordered to be constructed & to be tank proof.

 

PC

 

4

Training proceeded as per programme. The CO inspected each troop of the Bty during the morning. HQ fired on the open range. Rifle posts at road blocks ordered to be wired from bombing range, & to be protected from low flying aircraft. Min range at BHQ for all Ac’s. Work on road blocks still proceeding. Weather very fine.

 

PC

 

5

Morning training as per programme. Afternoon again spent on road blocks & collecting further material. In the evening 2nd I/C gave an instructive TEWT to all officers & senior NCOs of the battery, subject, orders & procedure for a mobile gun troop on imminent enemy landing. Return of Molotov bombs made to RHQ. On trial they have proved to be efficient & easily handled. Further operational instructions received Copy No 19. A concert was held in the evening. Further contact was made by BC with LDV Commanders. Weather continues very fine.

 

PC

 

6

Work was again carried on by D E & HQ Troops on the road blocks. F troop had their first drill order with the 25 pdrs in the afternoon, under the instruction of the IG. Officers attended RHQ for min range. Instructions were received for carrying out an exercise co-operating with the NDVF on Sat 8 Jun & 9 Jun 40. Weather again very fine.

 

PC

 

7

Training as per programme. F Troop did a drill order under the IG, occupying two positions in the morning & two in the afternoon. An advance & tank shoot were practised. Bren gun was again fired on open range by HQ troop. In the evening all officers & senior NCOs attended a lecture by Brigadier Miles, subject his experiences in Flanders & Dunkirk. This talk made a deep impression on all present. Weather very fine.

 

PC

 

8

At 0140 hrs message as per Appendix A attached was received. Training as per programme. In the morning officers & Ac’s attended lectures given by IG at RHQ. In the afternoon further road block construction was carried out.

 2 15 cwts (new) were received by the Bty. At 2115 hrs a practice against Parachutist landing was carried out at East Hoathly. E & F Troops took part in co-operation with LDV & Searchlight sections. Weather continues very hot & fine.

 

PC

 

9

HQ & F Troops held combined Church Parade at Warbleton Parish Church. D Troop at RHQ & E Troop at Horham Parish Church. Div. Commander ordered half holiday for troops except those on essential duties. Troops took the opportunity to go bathing. D Troop took part in parachutist landing practice in co-operation with LDV at 2115 hrs at Bodle Street. Weather fine.

 

PC

 

10

Training as per programme. The siting & digging of slit trenches was carried out at troop HQs with a view to both all round defence & protection from bombing. GPOAs & OPAs attended BHQ in the evening for miniature range. Weather very overcast & sultry.

 

PC

 

11

Training as per programme. F Troop did a drill order, occupying three different positions. In the evening, officers attended RHQ for min range. Weather again fine.

 

PC

 

12

In the morning 28 men from each Troop were detailed for baths in Eastbourne. Practice manning of roadblocks was carried out by HQ Troop. Further digging of slit trenches was carried out by Troops at Billets. Weather overcast.

 

PC

 

13

Training as per programme. F Troop had a drill order & again work was continued at Troop billets on the digging of slit trenches. E troop provided a party of 1 Officer 1 NCO & 10 men to act as parachutists, to land at 2030 hrs near Framfield & try to capture the telephone exchange, lorries & petrol pumps etc. The LDVF were detailed to protect these. All road blocks were ordered to be manned fully at 2000 hrs until 0700 hrs 14 Jun 40 & all cars & pedestrians to be stopped and questioned. The LDVF also took part in this. Weather overcast with very heavy thunder storm about 1100 hrs.

 

PC

 

14

Troops returned to Billets from Road Blocks at 0700 hrs & were given the morning off. In afternoon training went according to programme. Although several people were detained during the night 13/14 June 40 at Road Blocks their identities were eventually established. At approx 2030 hrs D Troop provided a force of 1 officer & 9 men to act as parachutists. They were dropped near Puddledock, objectives being the Post Office & filling station at Windmill Hill. The local LDV were turned out in force to prevent this. F Troop did a night occupation at Pekes House. The guns were laid out on their lines & the troop bivouacked there for the night.

 

PC

 

15

There was a general inspection of the Bty by Major Gen Schreiber DSO during the morning. He visited the troops in the following order: D Troop, HQ Troop, F Troop, E Troop. F Troop were in action doing a drill order following the previous night’s occupation. In the afternoon Signallers did a treasure hunt with wireless. Cricket matches were organised by Troops with local teams. At 2030 hrs HQ Troop provided a force of 1 officer & 10 men to act as parachutists. They landed in the area Punnetts Town objective filling station & telephone kiosk. Local LDVs were called out to prevent this. Weather very fine.

 

PC

 

16

HQ Troop & F Troop held combined Church Parade at Warbleton Parish Church, D Troop at RHQ & E Troop at Horham Church. Weather dull with rain.

 

PC

 

17

Training proceeded according to programme. Verbal orders were received that the equipment was to move. The 2nd I/C & BCs proceeded to the area allotted to the Regiment & reconnoitred for billets. These were found in Northiam & Vinehall Street. 384th Bty were allotted the Northiam Billets. The area was also reccied for troop positions & OPs for the tasks allotted to the 25 pdr troop. Weather fine.

 

PC

 

18

Training as per programme. Troops shot on the open rifle range with rifles & Bren gun. New weapon pits were constructed at Road Blocks. Officers min range at RHQ. Weather fine.

 

PC

 

19

More detailed reconnaissance of billets at Northiam was carried out & OPs & troop positions were selected. Written orders for the move were received in the evening. Training per programme was carried out by the Bty. At 2315 hrs Air raid warning red was received. All road blocks were at once fully manned.

 

PC

 

20

Stand down was ordered at 0415 hrs. Troops returned to billets & were ordered to rest during the morning. Pay parades in afternoon. Capt Boileau returned to duty from course at Larkhill & proceeded to E troop. Further reconnaissance with MO was made of new billets, & conference held with Troop Commanders re move. Weather fine.

 

PC

 

21

Further reconnaissance of new billets & final dispositions of troops decided on. Bty prepared for the move. F Troop were sent over during the day to make preparations for the occupation of billets by the rest of the Bty the next day. Weather fine.

 

PC

Northiam

22

The move was started at 0700 hrs & completed by 1200 hrs without incident. The rest of the day was spent in digging slit trenches & generally settling down. Major Chichester returned & took over command of the Bty.

 

PC

 

23

Church Parade for a service at the Parish Church Northiam. The Battery finished digging slit trenches round the Billeting area. Contact was made with LDV commanders at RHQ in the afternoon.

 

CC

 

24

D & E Troops were working on road blocks in the morning and afternoon. A practice parachute invasion was carried out with the local LDV. A very successful and instructive operation.

 

CC

 

25

D & E Troops working on road blocks in morning and afternoon. F Troop did gun drill in the morning. A fine day. An exercise with the Northiam and Beckley LDV.

 

CC

 

26

D & E Troops working on road blocks in morning and afternoon. F Tp did a drill order. A fine day.

 

CC

 

27

D & E Troops worked on road blocks in the morning and afternoon. F Troop reconnoitred for a permanent Troop position. A hot day.

 

CC

 

28

D and E Troop finished road blocks in the morning. Pay Parade and rifle instruction in the afternoon. A fine day.

 

CC

 

29

Inspections by Troop Commander in the morning. Half Holiday in the afternoon. There was a cricket match in Brickwall Park between D and F Tps. A fine day.

 

CC

 

30

Church Parade at the Parish Church Northiam in the morning. Capt Stoddart returned to duty from a course at Llandridnod Wells.
