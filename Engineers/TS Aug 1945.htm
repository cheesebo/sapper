Subject: Bridge No 153 over River Ems at Elburgen

CO 6 Rly C&M Gp RE

 

Reference your 6CM/21228/265 and 6CM/212228/266 both dated 27 Aug 1945 and conversation (Major Moncrieff – Major Millard) of 28 Aug 1945. It is noted that you wish this company to be responsible for the repairs of the above bridge, and that the work is to be completed by 21 Sept at the same time as the Leda Bridge, Leer.

 

As you are fully aware, the Road Bridge at Leer is the longest bridge to be constructed by any single unit in the NW European Theatre and has of necessity involved a great deal of organisation and hard work. To ensure the work being complete by the target date and maintain the smooth running of the job, shift work has had to be resorted, and the absolute maximum number of personnel have been working on the job, as is shown by the fact that German civilians are being employed for camp and cookhouse duties to relieve men for works.

 

To complete the works by 21 Sept this present organisation must go on, and for this reason I am forced, reluctantly, to protest against the work of reconstructing the Ems Bridge being added to this Company’s commitments at this present time. This latter job of patching up other peoples work is a complicated one which, in my opinion, cannot be rushed, and needs first class supervision and workmanship. I feel it is very unfair for the unit to be expected to provide this when all its resources are needed to give the first class job demanded on the Leda Bridge.

 

Unless shift work on the Leda Bridge is to be stopped and completion date, in consequence, retarded, additional personnel, plant and MT will be required, as is shown in the Appx to this letter. I feel very strongly also that a Staff Sgt from the Bridging Coy should live on the site and act as a general foreman, and that at least two officers should be with the detachment at Elburgen. As you are aware, a site for the camp is being recce at the moment. One low-loader (the 2nd being off the road) has today been sent to 107 TSD, Antwerp, to collect stores in accordance with your indent 6CM/B153/5, 7 & 8. Only a portion of these stores can be collected in one run, and the provision of the additional transport is, therefore, urgent. 3rd Cdn Div state they are not prepared to release transport to this Unit for works without the consent of 30 Corps.

 

You will note that my Schramm Compressor and RB 19 will not be available for the next two weeks at least, as they are fully employed on the Leda Bridge.

 

 

Leer Nr Emden              (Signed) F J Millard         Major RE

29 Aug 1945              OC 158th Railway Construction Company RE
